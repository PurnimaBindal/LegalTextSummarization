Hon'ble Shri Justice Sachin Singh Rajput JUDGMENT ON BOARD 13/07/2022

1. Assailing the legality, correctness, judicial propriety of the judgment dated 28/11/2019 passed in Sessions Trial No. 74/2017 by the Sessions Judge, Kondagaon, District Kondagaon (C.G.) by which the appellant was convicted for an offence under Section 307 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 (for short, ' IPC') and directed to undergo three years rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs.25,000/- and in default of payment of fine additional rigorous imprisonment for six months was added.

2. Prosecution story in brief is that the complainant was married with the appellant three years prior to the date of incident. The complainant Sangeeta Patel was working in Sharma Hotel with her father. One day appellant came to her house after consuming liquor and quarreled with her and since then the complainant was residing with her mother in Naveen Colony, Farasgaon. A day prior to the date of incident i.e. 10/02/2017 the appellant had come to the house of the complainant at Farasgaon and when she was having lunch, he asked her to accompany him to the Jungle for cutting of woods. Despite her refusal, the appellant forcibly took her to Patoda jungle and near Sai Temple he assaulted her with axe from sharp side. The axe was seized vide seizure memo (Ex.P/5). On account of the assault on her head and ears, she fell down on the ground. Even thereafter the appellant inflicted injuries with axe on her thigh (near her private part) and then ran away in the jungle with the axe. The injury was bleeding profusely. The victim somehow came to the road staggering where the passers-by shifted her to the Farasgaon Hospital by 108 Ambulance. On the intimation being given by the Community Health Center, Farasgaon, Ajay Jha (PW-4) recorded dehati nalishi (Ex.P/9) and thereafter she was medically examined. Thereafter on the basis of dehati nalishi (Ex.P/9) First Information Report (Ex.P/10) was registered at Crime No. 03/2017 against the accused/appellant under Section 307 of IPC. During the course of investigation, spot map was prepared, statements of the witnesses were recorded, blood stained soil and plain soil was seized from the place of incident, the statement of the complainant was recorded and cloths worn by her were seized. The axe seized under (Ex.P/5) on the memorandum of accused (Ex.P/4) was sent for examination to the doctor (PW-9), who while answering the query had stated that the injuries noticed by him could have been caused by the axe presented before him for examination. The medical examination report (Ex.P/13) shows incised wounds on the pinna of the ear, left temporal region, right temporal region and labia majora which were caused by hard and sharp object. According to the opinion given by the doctor (PW-9), injury No. 1 was grievous in nature and the rest are simple in nature. After completion of investigation, charge-sheet was filed before the competent Court and vide order dated 15/09/2017 the case was committed to the Sessions Court for trial. The appellant was charged for offence under Section 307 of IPC where he abjured his guilt and claimed for trial.

3. Prosecution has examined as many as 11 witnesses Hardik Shrivastava (PW-1), Krishna Patle (PW-2), Bhanupratap Yadav (PW-3), Ajay Jha (PW-4), Smt. Sangeeta Patel (PW-5), Chaitram (PW-6), Sukhiyarin Bai (PW-7), Smt. Jaishri Dhruv (PW-8), Dr. Lakhanlal Jurri (PW-9), Samluram Karanga (PW-10) and Bajaru Ram Pardhi (PW-11) to prove as the case and exhibited 14 documents. Statement of the appellant under Section 313 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (for short, ' CrPC') was also recorded in which he pleaded his innocence and false implication in the case. The appellant however did not examine any witness in support of his defence. The learned Sessions Court after appreciating the evidence brought before it convicted and sentenced the appellant as stated above. Hence, this appeal.

4. Counsel for the appellant vehemently argued that the impugned judgment of conviction and order of sentence passed by the learned trial Court is contrary to the evidence adduced by the prosecution and therefore not sustainable in law. He further submits that the learned trial Court has failed to appreciate the evidence in its proper perspective and erroneously passed the impugned judgment. He further submits that there are major omissions and contradictions in the statements of the witnesses which makes their statement untrustworthy and therefore no conviction can be based on such testimony. Counsel for the appellant further submits that even assuming that the appellant was the author of the crime, but looking to the injuries sustained by the complainant-injured and the evidence collected by the prosecution, the appellant cannot be convicted under Section 307 of IPC. Accordingly, the submission of counsel for the accused/appellant is that the accused/appellant is entitled for acquittal.

5. On the other hand learned State counsel supports the impugned judgment and submits that the findings recorded by the learned trial Court holding the accused/appellant guilty is based on proper appreciation of the evidence brought before it. He further submits that the prosecution by adducing the cogent and prudent evidence was able to bring home the guilt of the appellant. The medical evidence so collected by the prosecution and the statement of the complainant goes to show that it is the appellant who committed the crime in question and therefore the finding recorded by the trial Court convicting the appellant as aforesaid does not require any interference by this Court and therefore the appeal being without merits is liable to be dismissed.

6. I have heard learned counsel for the parties and considered the rival contentions raised at bar and also perused the records meticulously and with utmost circumspection.

7. The question which is to be determined by this Court is that whether on the basis of evidence adduced by the prosecution the learned Sessions Court was justified in convicting and sentencing the appellant as aforesaid? Complainant Smt. Sangeeta Patel (PW-5) is the injured and the sole eye-witness to the incident. In her statement in para 2, she has categorically stated that her husband (appellant) assaulted her with axe on ears, head and near the private parts because of which blood started oozing and she became unconscious. In paragraph 6 of her statement she has categorically denied that somebody from behind had assaulted her and because of which she had become unconscious. She further denied that the injury sustained by her is on account of falling from the tree. Though this witness was declared hostile and was cross-examined by the prosecution, however, meticulous examination of her entire statement, this witness remained firmed and her statement inspires confidence of this Court. Chaitram (PW-6), Suhkiyarin Bai (PW-7), Bajaru Ram Pardhi (PW-11) also corroborated the statement of complainant Smt. Sangeeta Patel (PW-5). Chaitram (PW-6) in paragraph 2 of his statement has stated that he along with his wife Sukhiyarin Bai took the complainant Smt. Sangeeta Patel for treatment to Kanker hospital where she told him that the appellant had assaulted her with axe in jungle. Though this witness has also been declared hostile, he appears to be a natural witness and there is no reason to disbelieve his version. Similar statement was also given by Sukhiyarin Bai (PW-7). The learned trial Court has in detail appreciated the testimony of the witnesses and came to a finding that it is the appellant who had caused the injuries to the complainant Smt. Sangeeta Patel. From the discussion above, this Court is of the opinion that the injured complainant Smt. Sangeeta Patel (PW-5), Chaitram (PW-6) and Sukhiyarin Bai (PW-7) their testimony inspire confidence and the conviction so recorded by the trial Court on the basis of their testimonies cannot be said to be perverse or illegal. Hence the finding recorded by the trial court convicting the appellant is hereby affirmed. Now the next submissions of the appellant with regard to the fact that the present is not a case of Section 307 of IPC. In order to appreciate the submission testimony of Dr. Lakhan Lal Jurri (PW-9) is important. PW-9 who has conducted the MLC of the injured Smt. Sangeeta Patel found following injuries :- (i) incised wound 1" x ½" Pinna of left ear hard and sharp (ii) incised wound 1" x ½" Left temporal region hard and sharp (iii) incised wound 1½ x1"x ½" Right temporal region hard and sharp (iv) 1"x ½" x ½" Right labia majora hard and sharp. He states that injury No. 1 was grievous in nature and rest of the injuries were simple in nature. He further states that in para No. 5 that the injuries sustained by the injured Smt. Sangeeta Patel may be caused from the seized axe. Query report is (Ex.P/14) and MLC is (Ex.P/13).

8. The statement of PW-9 also reveals that such injury can be caused by hard and sharp edged weapon and he also advised for X-ray and neurological and gynecological treatment. According to the X-ray report injury No. 1 was grievous and other injuries were simple in nature. He further states that injured was advised for treatment in the higher medical center. From perusal of the statement of PW-9 it appears that injury No. 1 was grievous in nature and rest of the injuries was simple in nature. This witness has not stated that the injured would have died if proper treatment was not given to her. It has come on record from the statement of Chaitram (PW-6) that on the advice of the doctor, the injured was taken to Kanker hospital where she was treated for about a week. On examination of the statement of Smt. Sangeeta Patel (PW-5) it appears she has not stated that the appellant caused injuries to her in order to kill her. On the contrary in para 4 she states that she does not know why her husband has assaulted. Except MLC (Ex.P/13) and evidence of Dr. Lakhan Lal Jurri (PW-9) no cogent evidence is available on record to substantiate that if the proper treatment was not given to the complainant, she would have died.

9. Section 307 of IPC reads as under :-

"307. Attempt to murder.--Whoever does any act with such intention or knowledge, and under such circumstances that, if he by that act caused death, he would be guilty of murder, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine; and if hurt is caused to any person by such act, the offender shall be liable either to 1[imprisonment for life], or to such punishment as is hereinbefore mentioned.
Attempts by life convicts.--2[When any person offending under this section is under sentence of 1[imprisonment for life], he may, if hurt is caused, be punished with death.] llustrations
(a) A shoots at Z with intention to kill him, under such circumstances that, if death ensued. A would be guilty of murder. A is liable to punishment under this section.
(b) A, with the intention of causing the death of a child of tender years, exposes it in a desert place. A has committed the offence defined by this section, though the death of the child does not ensue.
(c) A, intending to murder Z, buys a gun and loads it. A has not yet committed the offence. A fires the gun at Z. He has committed the offence defined in this section, and if by such firing he wounds Z, he is liable to the punishment provided by the latter part of 1[the first paragraph of] this section.
(d) A, intending to murder Z by poison, purchases poison and mixes the same with food which remains in A's keeping; A has not yet committed the offence defined in this section. A places the food on Z's table or delivers it to Z's servant to place it on Z's table. A has committed the offence defined in this section."

10. In order to sustain of conviction under Section 307 of IPC following ingredients is necessary as:-

The Hon'ble Supreme Court in the matter of Chimanbhai Jagabhai Patel Vs. State of Gujarat and another, (2009) 11 SCC 273 has held as under:-

"11. "20. ... The essential ingredients required to be proved in the case of an offence under Section 307 are:

(i) that the death of a human being is attempted;
(ii) that such death was attempted to be caused by, or in consequence of the act of the accused; and
(iii) that such act was done with the intention of causing death; or that it was done with the intention of causing such bodily injury as; (a) the accused knew to be likely to cause death; or (b) was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death, or that the accused attempted to cause by doing an act known to him to be so imminently dangerous that it must in all probability cause (a) death, or (b) such bodily injury as is likely to cause death, the accused having no excuse for incurring the risk of causing such death or injury."

11. Similarly, the Supreme Court in the matter of Jage Ram and others v.State of Haryana (2015) 11 SCC 366 has laid down the ingredients of the offence under Section 307 of the IPC and held as under: -

"12.For the purpose of conviction under Section 307 IPC, prosecution has to establish (i) the intention to commit murder; and (ii) the act done by the accused. The burden is on the prosecution that the accused had attempted to commit the murder of the prosecution witness. Whether the accused person intended to commit murder of another person would depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case. To justify a conviction under Section 307 IPC, it is not essential that fatal injury capable of causing death should have been caused. Although the nature of injury actually caused may be of assistance in coming to a finding as to the intention of the accused, such intention may also be adduced from other circumstances. The intention of the accused is to be gathered from the circumstances like the nature of the weapon used, words used by the accused at the time of the incident, motive of the accused, parts of the body where the injury was caused and the nature of injury and severity of the blows given, etc.

12. The trial Court came to a conclusion that the appellant has committed the said crime on the basis of injury sustained by the complainant and after assaulting the injured PW-5 he run away which goes to indicate that the dying declaration of PW-5 was also recorded and she remained in the hospital for the long period and on these basis the appellant was convicted for the offence under Section 307 of IPC. On deep analysis of the medical evidence available on record the following facts are reflecting :- four injuries was caused to the appellant, out of which incised wound 1" x ½" Pinna on left ear hard and sharp was grievous in nature and rest of the injuries were simple. The injured herself has stated that she was admitted in the Farasgaon hospital and subsequently in Kanker hospital where she remained under treatment for about seven days. It is to be seen that looking to the conditions of injured her dying declaration was recorded. The appellant left the injured after causing injuries to the injured. From the deep analysis of the entire evidence, intention of the appellant can be gathered.

13. In view of the above discussion, I do not find any illegality in the impugned judgment and appeal deserves to be and is hereby dismissed.
