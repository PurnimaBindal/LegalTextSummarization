                       J U D G M E N T

Delivered on 25th day of March 2010 WRIT PETITION UNDER ARTICLE 226 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA

1. Challenge in this petition is to the order dated 31.12.2007 (Annexure P/2) passed by the respondent No. 4, i.e. the Collector, Mahasamund, whereby the appointment of the petitioner alongwith 18 others by order dated 18.07.2007 (Annexure P/4) passed by the respondent No. 5 i.e. Chief Executive Officer, Janpad Panchayat, Basna, on the post of Shiksha Karmi Grade III, was cancelled, and further, the order dated 24.12.2008 (Annexure P/1) passed by the respondent No. 3, i.e. Director Panchayat, whereby the appeal preferred by the petitioner alongwith others, against the order dated 31.12.2007 (Annexure P/2) in Appeal Case No. 163/B.121/07.08, was rejected upholding the order passed by the Collector.

2. The brief facts, as projected by the petitioner are that pursuant to the advertisement dated 20.02.2007 (Annexure P/3) issued by the respondent No. 5, for selection and appointment on available vacancy of 89 posts of Shiksha Karmi Grade III, the petitioner alongwith others made an application. A select list of 81 candidates was declared on approval by the General Administration Committee. Out of 81 selected candidates, 38 persons joined the service on the basis of appointment orders. The petitioner was one of the 38 persons who also joined the service on the basis of appointment order dated 18.7.2007 (Annexure P/4). 43 posts remained vacant on account of non-joining of the remaining candidates. Though, there was no waiting list, a new list of 43 persons for appointment on the post of Shiksha Karmi Grade III was declared on 12.10.2007. Pursuant to the select list dated 12.10.2007, 10 persons joined and 33 persons again did not join. Further a list of 115 persons for appointment on the post of Shiksha Karmi Grade III was issued on 6.11.2007. On a complaint, a Committee was constituted by the Chief Executive Officer, Janpad Panchayat, Mahasamund to enquire into the appointments of Shiksha Karmi Grade III, pursuant to three different lists issued by the then Chief Executive Officer, Janpad Panchayat, Basna. In enquiry, subsequent list of 43 persons and 115 persons dated 12.10.2007 and 06.11.2007 respectively, were found defective and illegal. Accordingly, subsequent select lists of 43 persons and 115 persons were quashed on the basis of recommendation made by the Committee. On the basis of report submitted to the Collector under section 85(1) of the Panchyat Raj Adhiniyam, 1993, the respondent No. 4 cancelled the appointment of 19 persons including the petitioner also, who were appointed by order dated 18.7.2007 on the basis of first select list of 81 persons, without assigning any reason.

3. Thereagainst, the petitioner alongwith others preferred an appeal before the Director, Panchayat in appeal case No. 163/B.121/07.08. The Director, Panchayat found that the appointment of the petitioners alongwith others was not in accordance with law, on the basis of report submitted by the Committee to the Chief Executive Officer, Jila Panchayat, Mahasamund, without assigning any reason. The Director, in his order has not discussed any reason for cancelling the appointment of 19 Shiksha Karmis out of 38 Shiksha Karmis who were appointed vide the select list dated 18.07.2007. The subsequent select lists dated 12.10.2007 and 06.11.2007 were cancelled in their entirety. Thus, this petition.

4. Shri Pradhan, learned counsel appearing for the petitioner would submit that this is an individual case where basic principles of natural justice were required to be complied with as the petitioner has not admitted the fact that the documents was not proper or genuine.

5. Shri Pradhan would further submit that this is not a case of quashing of the selection on account of irregularities committed on mass scale. The petitioner was appointed pursuant to the first select list dated 18.07.2007. The petitioner obtained information under the Right to Information Act, 2000 whereunder it was found that the appointment of the petitioner was cancelled on the ground that the experience certificate, produced by the petitioner, of Jan Bhagidari School, Kolihadevri, Block Basna, was not proper, as his name was not found in any of the schools of Block Basna. Thus, the marks awarded to the petitioner on the basis of experience certificate was reduced. Further, the certificate of scout and guide was also cancelled as the same was not issued by the competent authority. Thus, the marks awarded to the petitioner was reduced from 65.36 to 50.86 and his appointment was accordingly cancelled.

6. Shri Shrivastava, learned counsel appearing for the respondent No. 5, on the other hand, basically justifies quashing of the select lists dated 12.10.2007 whereby 43 persons were appointed on the post of Shiksha Karmi Grade III and further the select list dated 06.11.2007 whereby 115 appointments were made. The counsel has not made any submission with regard to non-compliance of the principles of natural justice in case of the petitioner, except that the preliminary enquiry was made wherein all the concerned officers, who were responsible in conduct of the examination, were examined. But there is no whisper about opportunity of hearing to the petitioner.

7. Shri Roy, learned counsel appearing for the State/respondent No. 1 to 4 would adopt the submission made by the respondent No. 5 in oral argument as well as in their return.

8. Having heard learned counsel for the parties, perused the pleadings and documents appended thereto, it is evident that the enquiry committee in its report submitted to the Chief Executive Officer, Jila Panchayat, Mahasamund, has primarily dealt with the select list dated 12.10.2007 with respect to selection of 43 persons and select list dated 06.11.2007 with respect to selection of 115 persons on the post of Shiskah Karmi Grade III, which were found illegal as the same was published firstly without having any waiting list and secondly, certain approval as required under the provisions of law were not taken. In case of cancellation of appointment of the petitioner alongwith other 18 persons, there is neither any detail discussion in the enquiry report nor the Collector has discussed and considered anything in respect of the petitioner in his order dated 31.12.2007. The subsequent order of the Director, Panchayat in appeal, has also not given any reasons and has not adverted to the position as to why the principles of natural justice was dispensed with in case of the petitioner. It appears that the Officers below have been swayed by subsequent development in issuing select list dated 12-10-2007 and 6-11- 2007 for appointment on the post of Shiksha Karmi Grade - III, while considering the case of the petitioner. The petitioner has produced experience certificate (Annexure P/7) which appears to have been counter signed by the District Education Officer, Basna alongwith other signatories, and a copy of. Scout Guide certificate, which also appears to have been singed by the Block Scout Commissioner, Block Education Officer, Basna.

9. In reply filed by the respondent No. 5, it has not controverted any averments made by the petitioner except that the subsequent select list dated 12.10.2007 and 06.11.2007 was not approved by the General Administration Committee of Janpad Panchyat which was a mandatory as prescribed under Chhattisgarh Panchayat Shiksha Karmis (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1997. It is stated that the Committee has afforded opportunity to all the officers who were involved in the conduct of examination of appointment of Shiksha Karmi Grade III. But it is not the case of the respondent No. 5 that any opportunity of hearing was afforded to the petitioner.

10. This Court, in Mrityunjay Shukla & Others v. Municipal Corporation, Raipur & Others1, relying on Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan v. Mehbub Alam Laskar2, State of Punjab & Others v. Constable Avtar Singh (Dead) through LRs3, Ashok Kumar Sonkar v. Union of India & Others4, State of Manipur & Others v. Y.Token Singh & Others5, Jaswantsingh Pratap Singh Jadeja v. Rajkot Municipal Corporation & another6, Inderpreet Singh Khalon & Others v. State of Punjab & Others7, Mohd. Sartaj and another v. State of U.P. & Others8, Viveka Nand Sethi v. Chairman, J&K Bank Ltd. & Others9, Canara Bank & Others v. Debasis Das & Others10, Basudeo Tiwari v. Sido Kanhu University & Others11, D.K.Yadav v. J.M.A. Industries Ltd., & Others12 and Shrawan Kumar Jha & Others v. State of Bihar & Others13, decisions rendered by the Supreme Court, observed as under:

"21. There is a common thread that the principle of natural justice is not unruly horse. The principles of natural justice are required to be complied with, having regard to the facts situation obtained therein. Thus, the principles of natural justice may not be required to be followed in the cases, where the facts are admitted. Secondly, that it is practically impossible or highly improbable to afford an opportunity of hearing in the event of quashing of selection on account of irregularity committed on mass scale, or en-masse cancellation. Thirdly, no useful purpose would be served by affording an opportunity of hearing."

11. Applying the well settled principles of law to the facts of the case on hand where certain individuals have been taken out from a proper select list which was not vitiated in its entirety, and subsequently, even on enquiry on the ground of supply of forged documents, compliance of principles of natural justice is a must. It is well settled that the audi alteram partem is one of the basic pillars of natural justice which means no one should be condemned unheard {See : Ashok Kumar Sonkar (supra)}

12. For the reasons stated hereinabove, the order dated 24.12.2008 (Annexure P/1) and the order dated 31.12.2007 (Annexure P/2) in respect of the petitioner, are quashed. The matter is remitted back to the respondent No. 4, i.e. the Collector to consider and decide the case of the petitioner afresh, if so advised, after affording an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner to put forward his case and thereafter, pass appropriate order in accordance with law.

13. Resultantly, the writ petition is allowed to the above extent.

14. There shall be no order asto costs.

JUDGE
