ORAL
JUDGMENT

1. Heard learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani for petitioners and learned advocate Mr.R.C.Jani for respondents.

2. Present Civil Revision Application is filed by tenant. The HRP suit filed by landlord has been decreed against present tenant on the ground that tenant is in arrears of rent under Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act and thereafter, appeal preferred by tenant is also dismissed. The suit has been decreed against tenant on additional ground of having alternative accommodation as admitted by tenant which has been discussed by appellate Court while deciding Issue No.3.

3. The HRP Suit No.352 of 1983 was filed by landlord - Mohd. Iqbal and Mohd Usuf against present petitioners. The trial Court has decided civil suit partly allowing it with a direction to handover vacant possession of suit premises to plaintiffs by judgment and decree dated 16.1.1990. Thereafter, tenant had preferred Regular Civil Appeal No.77 of 1990 which has been decided by 2nd Joint District Judge, Ahmedabad Rural at Mirzapur vide Exh.74 on 18.10.1995 whereby appeal has been dismissed while confirming the judgment and decree passed by trial Court. The appellate Court has dismissed cross-objection filed by landlord.

4. Learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani for petitioners submitted that subordinate Courts committed an error in law in deciding the question that defendants - tenants are in arrears of rent for more than 6 months and entitled vacant possession of suit premises. He also submitted that both subordinate Courts have also committed error in law while coming to conclusion that defendants - tenants causing annoyance and nuisance to the neighbours. Before trial Court, defendants - tenants have filed written statement at Exh.9 denying claim of landlord. The petitioners have denied that they are in arrears of rent for a period of more than 6 months and also denied that they have acquired suitable alternative accommodation. The issue has been framed by trial Court vide Exh.10. Thereafter, evidence of landlord was recorded at Exh.54. The evidence of one Shri Suresh A. Patel was recorded at Exh.76 and evidence of one Shri Khan Abdulkalim Abdulhakim was recorded at Exh.82. The tenant was examined himself on oath vide Exh.99. After appreciating oral and documentary evidence and after hearing submissions of both learned advocates for both parties, trial Court come to conclusion that tenants are in arrears of rent for a period of more than 6 months and defendants - tenants have acquired the suitable alternative accommodation. However, trial Court has rejected the prayer of plaintiffs - landlords on the ground of conduct of defendants - tenants is such that it is a nuisance or annoyance to neighbours.

4.1 The appeal preferred by tenants - present petitioners are also dismissed. According to learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani, Courts below have erred in interpreting the provisions of Bombay Rent Act, more particularly Section 12(3)(a) and 12(3)(b) and Section 13(1)(l) of Rent Act. He also submitted that case of present petitioners - tenants governed under Section 12(3)(b) and not under Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act. The present petitioners - tenants are not in arrears of rent from 1.3.1982. He also submitted that facts have not been properly appreciated by trial Court that landlord had not accepted the rent paid by tenant for that particular period. Section 12(3)(b) of the Act does not cast obligation on the tenants to pay rent regularly. The trial Court has also not properly appreciated the fact that on the day on which issue has been framed, the tenant was not in arrears of rent. Therefore, tenant is having protection under provisions of Section 12(3)(b) of the Act. The tenant has not acquired suitable alternative accommodation within meaning of Section 13(1)(l) of the Act. Learned advocate Mr.Jani submitted that Courts below erred in not appreciating the fact that land which was allotted to petitioners was of 13 ft. to 10 ft. area and taking into consideration the strength of family members of tenant, rented premises is inadequate. Even newly acquired premises were not sufficient to accommodate all family members. Therefore, he submitted that Courts below have committed an error in law which requires interference by this Court while exercising powers under Section 29(2) of the Act.

5. Learned advocate Mr.R.C.Jani appearing on behalf of respondents - landlord submitted that Courts below have given concurrent finding of fact which has been admitted by tenant and there is no error of law committed by Courts below which requires interference by this Court while exercising powers under Section 29(2) of Rent Act.

6. The rent was fixed at Rs.20/- and Panchayat tax is to be paid by plaintiffs - landlord and rent was paid by tenant upto 28.2.1982. For that receipts have been given by plaintiffs. But subsequent period from 1.3.1982, rent was not paid by tenants in spite of repeated demand made by landlord. Therefore, on 21.1.1983 registered notice was served to tenant and it was received by tenant on 22.1.1983 but, that notice has not been implemented or acted upon by tenant and tenant was in arrears of rent beyond more than 6 months. Therefore, landlord is entitled to have the possession of rented premises. There was no dispute raised by defendants - tenants in respect to rent fixed by landlord at Rs.20/-. The issues have been framed by trial Court vide Exh.51. Therefore, learned advocate Mr.R.C.Jani submitted that finding given by trial Court in respect to factual aspect, in affirmative in favour of landlord, against which no rebuttal evidence has been produced by tenants. Certain documents have been produced by tenants. Vide Exh.58, reply of notice was given by defendants - tenants and defendants - tenants were examined at Exh.99 and thereafter, pursis was filed by both learned advocates before trial Court in respect to deposited amount of rent with a date of deposit vide Exh.102. At the time when the matter was heard by trial Court, no advocate remained present on behalf of tenants and no arguments have been made by advocate on behalf of tenants. The matter was heard by trial Court after considering submissions made by learned advocate Mr.G.B.Shah, who is appearing on behalf of landlord. He submitted that monthly tenancy and rent was fixed at Rs.20/- and no dispute about standard rent has been raised by tenants vide Exh.32 giving reply before trial Court. The rent was remained due w.e.f. 1.3.1982. Therefore, trial Court has examined the question of fact whether rent remained due for about 12 months from 1.3.1982 to 28.2.1983 or not. This aspect has been considered by trial Court on the basis of evidence on record and no dispute has been raised by tenant within period of one month after receiving notice from landlord. Therefore, learned advocate Mr.R.C.Jani submitted that in evidence of defendants - tenants vide Exh.99, fact of paying rent to landlord has not been established or proved because no money order has been sent to the landlords by tenants and no such documents have been produced by defendant in his evidence. On the contrary, tenants have admitted in his evidence that they were not having knowledge as to on which date, money order was sent to landlords. No witness was examined by tenants in support of his case that money order was sent to landlord. The defendants - tenants have not raised any contention about standard rent after receiving notice from landlord and rent was not paid within a period of one month from date of receiving notice from landlord. Therefore, learned advocate Mr.R.C.Jani submitted that because of concurrent finding given by Courts below as well as clear admission made by defendants - tenants before appellate Court while deciding Issue No.3 having alternative accommodation acquired, therefore, no interference is required because no error of law committed by Courts below under Section 29(2) of Rent Act.

7. I have considered submissions made by both learned advocates and also perused judgment and decree passed by trial Court as well as appellate Court. I have also perused record and proceedings received from Courts below. The trial Court has decided issues which have been framed vide Exh.51 that notice issued by landlord is valid and legal and tenants were in arrears of rent covered by Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act. The appellate Court has considered relevant provisions of Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act which required that rent is payable by month, there is no dispute regarding standard rent or permitted increases and arrears of rent or increase is for a period of six months or more, for that notice for determination of tenancy and demanding arrears under Section 12(2) is served and tenants have neglected to pay arrears of rent or increase though one month after notice period has expired. Therefore, according to Courts below, case of landlord is covered by Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act and on that basis, decree was passed by trial Court.

7.1 It is necessary to note that according to case of plaintiffs - landlord, who has to pay taxes in respect to suit property, that fact has not been disputed by tenants. The reasoning given by appellate Court while deciding Point Nos.1, 2 and 3 where appellate Court has discussed the question as to whether case of tenants has been covered by either Section 12(3)(a) or 12(3)(b) of Rent Act or not ? The reasoning given by appellate Court in Para.14 and 15 are quoted as under :

"14. At the first instance, it will have to be decided that whether Sec.12(3)(a) or Sec.12(30(b) of the Rent Act would be attracted in the facts of our present case and after deciding that fact, it would be easy to resolve the controversy posed for decision, and a faint attempt has been made by Shri N.K.Jayswal, the learned advocate appearing for the appellant to convince that Sec.12(3)(a) of Rent Act would be attracted in the facts of present case and to reinforce his argument he referred and relied on the reported decision of Hon'ble Gujarat High Court in the case of Somabhai Kalidas Patel v. Bachubhai Sankalchand Modi, 1986 GLH (UJ) 22, wherein it has been held that when the defendant or a tenant has to pay the taxes over and above the rent, the case will not fall under Sec.12(3)(a) of the Rent Act but, for a simple reason, I am of the opinion that the decision cited at the bar is of no avail to the case of the defendant because nowhere it is the case of the plaintiff that it is the defendant who has to pay taxes in respect of the suit property and a specific case has been pleaded in plaint Exh.1 that it is the plaintiff - landlord who has to pay taxes in respect of the suit property and therefore, in the present case, it is clear that only Section Sec.12(3)(a) of the Rent Act would be attracted.

15. It is a settled law that the following conditions are required to be satisfied in a suit governed by clause (a) and they are -

(1)

rent is payable by month;

(2)

there is no dispute regarding standard rent or permitted increases;

(3)

arrears of rent or increase is for a period of six months or more;

and so far the first condition is concerned as such, it is not the case of the defendant that the rent is not payable by month and in his deposition Exh.99 also as such there is no controversy or dispute so far rent is payable by month is concerned and as such there is no dispute regarding the standard rent or permitted increases because in his written statement vide Exh.9, Para.4 he has admitted in so many words that there is no dispute on the point that the rent of the property is Rs.20/- per month. No so far as the question of rent for a period of six months or more is concerned, according to the case of the plaintiff, the defendant - tenant has paid rent upto 28.2.82 and thereafter, from 1.3.82 to 28.2.83 the rent is due from the defendant - tenant and as such it is also not in dispute that the defendant tenant has been served with registered post notice vide Exh.57 wherein it has been stated that he is a tenant in arrears of rent from 1.3.82 and it is not in dispute that the deft. has been served with the notice vide Exh.57 and it is also not in dispute that he has not sent the amount claimed in notice within a month after the notice period has expired and the only defence that has been put forth by the defendant is that the plaintiff was not deliberately accepting the rent or that to corner him he was not issuing the receipt deliberately but, in that connection it is necessary to refer the deposition of the defendant - Bahadursha Dadusha vide Exh.99, and the defendant has admitted in his cross-examination that

"Point No.3:

8. In light of this reasoning given by appellate Court, after appreciating evidence led before trial Court, the contentions raised by learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani cannot be accepted because ingredients of Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act is fully satisfied by plaintiffs. After receiving notice from landlord, tenants have not raised dispute about standard rent and panchayat tax has been paid by plaintiffs. Therefore, it was a monthly tenancy and after receiving notice from landlord, for more than 12 months rent has not been paid to landlord by tenants and that rent become due and not paid within one month from date of receiving notice from plaintiffs. Therefore, Courts below have rightly considered Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act and for that, landlord is entitled to recover possession from tenant. The alternative accommodation is also admitted by tenants in Exh.99. Therefore, when Courts below arrived and having concurrent finding of fact decided, this Court is having very limited jurisdiction to interfere with finding of fact recorded by Courts below. This aspect has been examined by this Court in case of Urvashiben Kanubhai Joshi wd/o Kanubhai K. Joshi v. Bakshisinh Ishwarsinh, reported in 2009 (2) GLR 1078. This Court has also decided question that under Section 29(2) of Rent Act, High Court would not re-appreciate the evidence in exercise of its limited jurisdiction under Section 29(2) of Rent Act in case of Bhavarlal Ganeshram v. Jasiben wd/o Ishwarlal Ramchand and Others, reported in 2006 (3) GLR 2230. The relevant observations are in Para.8 which is quoted as under :

"Both the courts below have concurrently found that no decree for possession can be granted on the aforesaid ground of permanent construction. Mr. Juneja is not in a position to point out as to how the finding of fact recorded by the Courts below is contrary to law. In its limited jurisdiction under section 29 (2) of the At, this Court is not expected to re-appreciate the evidence on record. Considering the reasonings of the courts below, I do not find any substance in the arguments of Mr. Juneja that decree for possession was required to be passed for erection of permanent structure as provided under section 13 (1)(b) of the Act."

9. The Apex Court has also considered question of limited jurisdiction in Revision Application preferred under Section 29(2) of the Rent Act in case of Patel Valmik Himatlal and Others v. Patel Mohanlal Muljibhai, reported in (1998) 7 SCC 383. The relevant observations are in Para.4, 5 and 6 which is quoted as under :

"4. Section 29(2) of the Bombay Rents Act as applicable to Gujarat amendment reads as follows :-

"29(2).

No further appeal shall lie against any decision in appeal under sub-section (1) but the High Court may, for the purpose of satisfying itself that any such decision in appeal was according to law, call for the case pass such order with respect thereto as it thinks fit."

5. The ambit and scope of the said section came up for consideration before this Court in Helper Girdharbhai vs. Saiyed Mohmad Mirasaheb Kadri and others: (1987) 3 SCC 538 and after referring to a catena of authorities, Sabyasachi Mukharji, J. drew a distinction between the appellate and the revisional jurisdictions of the courts and opined that the distinction was a real one. It was held that the right to appeal carries with it the right of rehearing both on questions of law and fact, unless the statute conferring the right to appeal itself limits the rehearing in some way, while the power to hear a revision is generally given to a particular case is decided according to law. The Bench opined that although the High Court had wider powers than that which could be exercised under Section 115 of the Code of Civil Procedure, yet its revisional jurisdiction could only be exercised for a limited purpose with a view to satisfying itself that the decision under challenge before it is according to law. The High Court cannot substitute its own findings on a question of fact for the findings recorded by the courts below on reappraisal of evidence. Did the High Court exceed its jurisdiction ?

6. The powers under section 29(2) are revisional powers with which the High Court is clothed. It empowers the High Court to correct errors which may make the decision contrary to law and which errors go to the root of the decision but it does not vest the High Court with the power to re-hear the matter and re-appreciate the evidence. The mere fact that a different view is possible on re-appreciation of evidence cannot be a ground for exercise of the revisional jurisdiction."

10. In view of above observations made by this Court as well as Apex Court, contentions raised by learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani cannot be accepted because this Court cannot now re-appreciate the evidence which was already appreciated by trial Court as well as appellate Court. In this case, Courts below have not committed any error of law and rightly examined matter which covered by Section 12(3)(a) of Rent Act and tenant was in arrears about more than 6 months' period having monthly tenancy and there is an admission of tenant vide Exh.99 in his evidence that he acquired alternative accommodation where he is residing with his sons. Therefore, contentions raised by learned advocate Mr.P.K.Jani cannot be accepted and same are rejected. According to my opinion, no error of law is committed by Courts below and decision of Courts below are not contrary to law. Therefore, there is no substance in present Civil Revision Application. Accordingly, present Civil Revision Application is dismissed. Rule is discharged. Interim relief, if any, granted earlier stands vacated. No costs.
