       Heard. 

       Order dated 8.3.2013 is being assailed by the petitioners; 
whereby,   claim   for   regularization   as   Waterman   and   Pump 
Operator has been rejected.

       The   impugned  order  has  been  passed  in  pursuance   to 
direction in Writ Petition No. 11832/2012, which was filed by 
the petitioner for direction to Collector, Tikamgarh to permit 
them to sign attendance register, pay the wages at Collector's 
rate and for regularization as Waterman/Pump Operator.

       As evident from the impugned order the claim has been 
rejected on the ground that while engaged as part­time daily 
wager, no procedure for recruitment was followed.

       Facts

  unfurled   from   the   pleadings   reveal   that   the  petitioner   No.   1   was   engaged   as   part   time   Pump   Operator/  Waterman on daily wages at Collector's rate on 8.8.1996; that  his services were utilized as a Waterman.  Petitioner No. 2 was  engaged as part time waterman by order dated 23.9.1993 on  daily   wages   at   Collector's   rate   for   period   of   89   days.     The  engagement of the petitioners as part time daily wager were  extended from time to time.

The   services   of   the   petitioners   were   dispensed   with   in  September   2000.     Petitioners   challenged   their   termination  2 before   the   Madhya   Pradesh   Administrative   Tribunal   vide  Original   Application   No.   3032/2000   which   was   later   on  transferred to the High Court after closure of the Tribunal and  registered as W.P. No. 15990/2003 (A).   This writ petition was  disposed of on 8.5.2004 in the following terms:

"In view of change in the policy decision, petitioner  is   directed   to   be   reinstated,   however,   without   back­ wages.     Petitioner   shall   be   entitled   for   the   benefit   of  continuity in service and other benefits as envisaged in  the consent order passed in L.P.A 247/2002."

  The   L.P.A  247/2002   which  has   been  referred   to   was  in  fact settled in the Lok Adalat where the parties had agreed to  following terms on 31.1.2004:

"In view of the aforesaid, L.P.A stands disposed of in  the following terms:­
(a) The employees of the respondent ­ Union shall be  taken   back   in   service   in   a   phase   wise   manner  within a period of six months.
(b) Employees   be   conferred   the   benefit   of   notional  continuity   so   that   they   are   not   deprive   of   their  right to be regularized, if and When a scheme is  framed by the State Government for that purpose.
(c) Whether the issue arises at any point of time for  extending   the   benefit   of   promotion   or   ex­gratia  payment   of   the   service,   the   notional   continuity  would   be   counted   in   favour   of   the   employees.  Needless to emphasize, a scheme is brought into  force  for grant  of pension or ex­gratia payment,  3 there   shall   be   a   computation   of   the   period  treating   it   as   notional.   That   would   mean   or  deemed to mean that the qualifying service would  not carry financial component along with it.  It is  hereby made clear that both the parties very fairly  state that any settlement with regard to framing of  pension   scheme   in   future   or   conferring   the  benefit of regularization by way of policy decision  would   confer   a   justifiable   right   on   them,   as  warranted in law.
(d) The   employees   shall   withdraw   all   the   cases  pending   in various   legal  forums   before  they  are  extended the benefit of appointment.
(e) The employees shall fill up the form given by the  State   Government,   which   would   not   contradict  any part of this order.

Pausing   for   a   moment   at   this   stage,   in   respect   of  settlement arrived at in a Lok Adalat and a direction thereon in  Writ Petition 15990/2003 (S) would not reap any benefit in view  of   the   law   laid   down   in   Secretary,   State   of   Karnataka   v.  Umadevi {(2006) 4 SCC 1} wherein it has been held "54. It is also clarified that those decisions which run counter to the principle settled in this decision, or in which directions running counter to what we have held herein, will stand denuded of their status as precedents."

Be  that,  as  it   may.    The  petitioners  were  reinstated  by  order dated 21.9.2005.  The reinstatement as apparent by order  4 was   conditional   subject   to   outcome   of   the   Special   Leave  Petition and certain other conditions stipulated therein.

The Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) 3462/2006 preferred  against   the   order   dated   8.5.2004   in   W.P.   No.   15990/2002   by  State of Madhya Pradesh was dismissed on 21.8.2006 in view of  the   remedy   of   intra   Court   appeal   being   available   with   the  enactment   of   Madhya   Pradesh   Uchcha   Nyayalay   (Khand  Nyaypeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005.  The State of Madhya  Pradesh was set at liberty to avail the said remedy.

That,   by   order   dated   1.7.2009,   4.5.2010,   16.5.2011  petitioner's   services   as   part   time   Waterman   and   Pump  Operator   and   daily   wages   was   extended   from   time   to   time  subject to stipulations therein.

That,   after   the   decision   in   Umadevi   (supra),   State  Government  framed  a  policy   of one  time   regularization  vide  General Administration Department Circular No. F­5­3/2004/  ,d/3   Bhopal,   dated   15/5/2007.     Petitioners   applied   for  regularization of their services.   When the representation did  not   reap   any   response,   petitioners   filed   W.P.   1183/2012   (S),  which   was   disposed   of   on   22.1.2013   with   direction   to  respondents to consider the claim.

The representation was considered and negatived by the  impugned order dated 8.3.2013 in the following terms:

The correctness of the order being questioned, the issue  which   crops   up   for   consideration   is   whether   the   authority  concerned has committed any illegality as would warrant any  indulgence.

6

In State of U.P. v. Neeraj Awasthi [(2006) 1 SCC 685] it is observed by their Lordships:

"52 When questioned, Mr. Chaudhari and Mr. Sanghi submitted that regularization would mean permanence. Regularization of the services of an employee would, therefore, mean that the persons concerned who had no status within the purview of the definition of "employee" would become employees. Thus, a change in the status would be effected.
53. An attempt to induct an employee without following the procedure would be a back-door appointment. Such back-door appointments have been deprecated by this Court times without number. (Seen for example Delhi Development Horticulture Employees Union v. Delhi Admn, SCC para 23).
55. A three-Judge Bench of this Court upon taking into consideration a large number of decisions in A. Umarani v. Registrar, Coop. Societies - (2004) 7 SCC 112, held that illegal appointments cannot be regularized. It was further held: (SCC P. 126, para 45) "45. No regularization is, thus, permissible in exercise of the statutory power conferred under Article 162 of the Constitution if the appointments have been made in contravention of the statutory rules."
7

59. In Mahendra L. Jain v. Indore Development Authority - (2005) 1 SCC 639 it was categorically held: (SCC p. 649, para 19) "19. The question, therefore, which arises for consideration is as to whether they could lay a valid claim for regularization of their services. The answer thereto must be rendered in the negative. Regularisation cannot be claimed as a matter of right. An illegal appointment cannot be legalized by taking recourse to regularization. What can be regularized is an irregularity and not an illegality. The constitutional scheme which the country has adopted does not contemplate any back-door appointment. A State before offering public service to a person must comply with the constitutional requirements of Articles 14 and 16 of the Constitution. All actions of the State must conform to the constitutional requirements. A daily-wager in the absence of a statutory provision in this behalf would not be entitled to regularization. (See State of U.P. v. Ajay Kumar and Jawaharlal Nehru Krishi Vishwa Vidyalaya v. Bal Kishan Soni.).

8

61. It is, therefore, not correct to contend that only because in the correspondences between the State and the Board the appointments of such persons have been described to be irregular, the same would not mean that they are not illegal.

62. In any event, no temporary or permanent status can be granted to an employee by way of regularization. (See Union of India v. Gagan Kumar and State of Maharashtra v. R.S. Bhonde.)

70. On the other hand, in a series of decisions, which we have noticed hereinbefore, this Court has now firmly laid down the law that regularization cannot be a mode of appointment."

The   impugned   order   when   adjudged   on   the   anvil   of  principles of law laid down in Secretary, State of Karnataka  v.  Umadevi, State of U.P. v. Neeraj Awasthi, Mahendra L. Jain v.  Indore Development Authority (supra and in the light of facts  that  the petitioner's were  only  daily wagers engaged on part  time   basis   without   following   any   procedure   of   recruitment  known   to   law,   the   decision   rendered   by   the   competent  authority cannot be faulted with.

In the result petition fails and is dismissed.  No costs.
