1. Through the medium of instant petition filed under Article 227 of the Constitution of India, petitioner herein seeks quashing of order dated 19.07.2021 passed by the court of Judge Small Causes, Srinagar and order dated 26.08.2021 (for short the impugned orders) passed by the 3rd Additional District Judge, Srinagar, (for short „the trial court‟ and „the appellate court‟ respectively).

2. Before adverting to the grounds of challenge urged in the petition, brief facts under the cover of which the petitioner has filed the instant petition are that a suit for declaration and specific performance of contract is stated to have been filed by the respondent herein before the trial court on 20.04.2021 wherein ex-parte interim order dated 20.04.2021 is stated to have been passed by the trial court in the application for interim relief accompanying the suit. The said application for interim relief is stated to have been disposed of by the trial court in terms of order dated 19.07.2021 while making the order dated 20.04.2021 absolute.

3. Aggrieved of the order dated 19.07.2021, an appeal is stated to have been preferred by the petitioner herein before the appellate court which appeal is stated to have been disposed of, upholding order dated 19.07.2021 passed by the trial court and further providing that since counsel for the appellant/petitioner herein has raised issue regarding maintainability/jurisdiction of the suit before the trial court, as such, the trial court is directed to frame preliminary issue in this context and return its finding, preferably within a period of one month.

4. The impugned orders are being assailed by the petitioner inter-alia on the grounds that same are bad in law and that plaintiff/respondent had no right/locus to maintain the suit being barred under Section 14(c) and Section 41 (e) of Specific Relief Act and thus, the trial court lack jurisdiction to try the suit and as such, could not have passed the impugned order and that in the application for interim relief consequently interim relief could not have been passed in view of the aforesaid bar and that the trial court in essence granted final relief while keeping in abeyance notice dated 16.04.2021 and that the trial court did not appreciate the principles of prima-facie case, balance of convenience and irreparable loss in its true spirit and that the trial court passed impugned order erroneously while imaginary linking the loan agreement with lease agreement though same being distinct and different from each other and that the courts below lost sight of the fact that the impugned orders would result in grave miscarriage of justice being detrimental to the petitioner bank which holds public money and that the impugned orders resulted in abuse process of law causing serious miscarriage of justice and that the courts below failed to take notice of willful suppression of material on the part of respondent herein and that the courts below passed the orders in contradiction of their own observations while holding on one hand that the primary business of the respondent is a hotel business being conducted in the premises in question and on the other hand making the base that the primary security given in the loan agreements separately executed in the year 2018 is the rentals of the premises which are being paid by the petitioner bank and that there appears to be a link between the lease agreement and the loan agreement least carrying that both are different and executed on different dates and providing for different set of rights and obligations and that under Article 227, the High court having powers of superintendence over all the Subordinate courts is vested with the power of judicial review for correcting judicial errors and therefore, in the present case power under Article 227 of the Constitution is warranted to be exercised as the courts below have passed impugned order in a suit which is barred by law.

5. Learned appearing counsel for the petitioner while making his submissions reiterated the contentions raised and grounds urged in the petition and prayed for setting aside of the impugned orders, whereas, per-contra learned counsel for the caveator/respondent herein would controvert and resist the contentions raised and grounds urged by the petitioner‟s counsel and while defending the impugned orders would contend that the petition is misconceived and is not maintainable in the facts and circumstances of the case.

6. Heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the record.

7. Before proceeding to advert to the issues involved in the petition, it would be appropriate and advantageous to refer to the nature, scope and object of injunctions enshrined in Order 39 of CPC as under:-

Rule 1 of Order 39 of the Code enables a court to grant temporary injunction in the following cases:
(i) Where the disputed property is in danger of being wasted, damaged or alienated by any party to the suit, or is wrongfully sold in execution of a decree;
(ii) Where the defendant threatens, or intends to remove or dispose of his property with a view to defrauding his creditors;
(iii) Where the defendant threatens to dispossess the plaintiff or otherwise cause injury to the plaintiff in relation to the disputed property.
Rule 1 of Order 39 has to be read with Rule 2 of Order 39 as also clause (c) of Section 94. So temporary injunction can also be granted in the following cases:
(i) Where the defendant is about to commit breach of contract, or cause other injury (Or.39, R.2);
(ii) Where grant of temporary injunction is necessary to prevent the ends of justice being defeated [S.94(c)].

An interim or temporary injunction/relief is a judicial remedy by which a person is ordered to refrain from doing or to do a particular act or thing. The primary purpose of granting interim relief is the preservation of property in dispute till legal rights and conflicting claims of the parties before the court are adjudicated.

The object of making an order of interim relief is to evolve a workable formula required in a particular situation, keeping in mind pros and cons of the matter while striking a delicate balance between two conflicting interests. The underlying object of grant temporary injunction is to maintain and preserve the lis/subject matter and to prevent any change in it until the final determination of the suit. An injunction may be in a restrictive form or a mandatory form.

The Apex court in case titled as "Dalpat Kumar and another Vs. Prahlad Singh and Others, reported in 1992 (1) SCC 719, being relevant and germane herein at Para 4, & 5, restated the principles qua grant of interim injunctions as follows: -

"4........................Injunction is a judicial process by which a party is required to do or to refrain from doing any particular act. It is in the nature of preventive relief to a litigant to prevent future possible injury. In other words, the court in exercise of the power of granting ad interim injunction is to preserve the subject matter of the suit in the status quo for the time being. It is settled law that the grant of injunction is a discretionary relief.

The exercise thereof is subject to the court satisfying that (1) there is a serious disputed question to be tried in the suit and that an act, on the facts before the court, there is probability of his being entitled to the relief asked for by the plaintiff/defendant; (2) the court's interference is necessary to protect the party from the species of injury. In other words, irreparable injury or damage would ensue before the legal right would be established at trial; and (3) that the comparative hardship or mischief or inconvenience which is likely to occur from withholding the injunction will be greater than thatwould be likely to arise from granting it.

5. Therefore, the burden is on the plaintiff by evidence aliunde by affidavit or otherwise that there is "a prima facie case" in his favour which needs adjudication at the trial. The existence of the prima facie right and infraction of the enjoyment of his property or the right is a condition for the grant of temporary injunction. Prima facie case is not to be confused with prima facie title which has to be established, on evidence at the trial. Only prima facie case is a substantial question raised, bona fide, which needs investigation and a decision on merits. Satisfaction that there is a prima facie case by itself is not sufficient to grant injunction. The Court further has to satisfy that non-interference by the Court would result in "irreparable injury" to the party seeking relief and that there is no other remedy available to the party except one to grant injunction and he needs protection from the consequences of apprehended injury or dispossession. Irreparable injury, however, does not mean that there must be no physical possibility of repairing the injury, but means only that the injury must be a material one, namely one that cannot be adequately compensated by way of damages. The third condition also is that "the balance of convenience" must be in favour of granting injunction. The Court while granting or refusing to grant injunction should exercise sound judicial discretion to find the amount of substantial mischief or injury which is likely to be caused to the parties, if the injunction is refused and compare it with that it is likely to be caused to the other side if the injunction is granted. If on weighing competing possibilities or probabilities of likelihood of injury and if the Court considers that pending the suit, the subject-matter should be maintained in status quo, an injunction would be issued. Thus the Court has to exercise its sound judicial discretion in granting or refusing the relief of ad interim injunction pending the suit.

Further Apex court in case titled as "Gujarat Bottling Co. Ltd. vs. Coca Cola Co. reported in 1995 (5) SCC 545, observed following: -

In the leading case of Gujarat Bottling Co. Ltd. V. Coca Cola co., the Apex Court observed that the decision whether or not to grant an interlocutory injunction has to be taken at a time when the existence of the legal right assailed by the plaintiff and its alleged violation are both contested and uncertain and remain uncertain till they are established at the trial on evidence. Relief by way of interlocutory injunction is granted to mitigate the risk of injustice to the plaintiff during the period before that uncertainty could be resolved. The object of the interlocutory injunction is to protect the plaintiff against injury by violation of his right for which he could not be adequately compensated in damages recoverable in the action if the uncertainty were resolved in his favour at thetrial."

A further reference to the judgement of the Apex court passed in "Colgate Palmolive (India) Ltd V. Hindustan Lever Ltd., reported in 1999 (7) SCC 1, wherein at Para 24, being relevant and germane herein, the Apex court laid down as under:-

"24.We, however, think it fit to note herein below certain specific considerations in the matter of grant of interlocutory injunction, the basic being non-expression of opinion as to the merits of the matter by the court, since the issue of grant of injunction, usually, is at the earliest possible stage so far as the time-frame is concerned."

8. In view of the aforesaid legal position and the principles laid down by the Apex court, the validity or otherwise the impugned orders is required to be tested, having regard to the case setup in the petition.

9. Perusal of the record would tend to show that the respondent herein in the suit in question has sought a decree for declaring the notice dated 16.04.2021 as null and void with the consequential relief for directing the defendant petitioner herein to comply with the terms of the contract and complete 15 years and to ensure further liquidation of loan linked with rent account on the premise, that the petitioner and respondent had entered into an agreement on 19.04.2010 for a period of 15 years whereunder the plaintiff being the owner of property agreed to lease hotel namely "Hotel Shanhenshah Palace" situated at Dalgate, upto 3500 sqfts in the ground floor and that in furtherance of the contract obligation modified his estate making available additional space for making the terms of the agreement workable and in this regard the loan account linked with rent account was made operational so as to deduct the rent paid towards the plaintiff/respondent herein in the loan account and that the defendant/petitioner herein complied with the terms of agreement for 10 years, but proceeded illegally and terminated lease agreement in terms of notice dated 16.04.2021, while violating the express provisions of lease agreement wherein the bank was supposed to pay in advance amount equivalent to 6 months rent payable during the Block of 05 years at a start of each Block of lease period for adjusting and appropriating against 50% of monthly rent payable during the last 5 years of relative Block of the respective Block as the bank had proceeded to enter into the least Block of 5th year, as such, the bank was estopped from terminating the terms of the contract.

10. Perusal of the record would reveal that in response to the notice issued by the trial court, defendant/petitioner herein filed response and raised a preliminary objection about the maintainability of the suit on the basis of Section 14 (c) read with Section 41 (e) of the Specific and Relief Act.

11. Perusal of the record would further reveal that the trial court while taking notice of the said objection raised by the defendant/petitioner herein opined that the said objection can be taken as an issue for final disposal of the suit and not while deciding the application for interim relief and thus proceeded to decide the application for interim relief.

12. Perusal of the record in general and impugned orders in particular would reveal and suggest that the trial court rightly refrained from undertaking and exercise qua the merits of case which is forbidden in law while deciding an application for interim relief as law is no more res-integra that while considering an application for interim relief, the trial court has not to hold a mini trial, but has to follow the mandate enshrined in Order 39 CPC coupled with the golden and settled principles of prima-facie case, balance of convenience and irreparable loss. The trial court has thus, rightly proceeded to dispose of the application keeping in mind the said mandate and the said principles, leaving it open for dealing with question of maintainability of the suit to be decided upon framing of the issue. The trial court thus, cannot be said to have faulted while passing the impugned order dated 19.07.2021.

13. The appellate court as well while considering the appeal filed by the petitioner herein has rightly upheld the order passed by the trial court in terms of impugned order dated 26.08.2021. The power of appellate court while testing the validity or otherwise is circumscribed and is not to be interfered unless same is perverse or against the settled principle of law. The appellate court has declined to interfere with the order of trial court fundamentally and rightly on the ground that question of maintainability/jurisdiction of the suit can be adjudicated upon framing of a preliminary issue preferably within a stipulated period of one month. The appellate court as well cannot by any sense of imagination be said to have faulted while passing the impugned order dated 26.08.2021 which has agreed with the reasoning given by the trial court in respect of grant of interim relief.

14. The settled position of law is that High courts in exercise of Supervisory Jurisdiction can neither on the drop of a hat in exercise of its power under Article 227 of the Constitution interfere with the orders of courts inferior to it, nor can it act as a court of appeal over the orders of the subordinate courts and also High Court cannot under the said jurisdiction interfere to correct mere errors of law or fact or just because another view than the view taken by the subordinate court is a possible view. The jurisdiction under Article 227 of the Constitution has to be exercised very sparingly and has not to be exercised just for grant of relief in individual case. An improper and a frequent exercise of power under Article 227 of the Constitution has been held by the Apex court to be counter-productive.

15. Having regard to what has been observed, considered and analyzed hereinabove, impugned orders does not call for any interference in exercise of Supervisory Jurisdiction under Article 227 of the Constitution of this court. Resultantly petition fails and is dismissed.

16. It is made clear that nothing herein above shall be construed to be expression of any opinion about the merits of the case.
