Patna High Court - Orders
Ram Swaroop Mahto vs Sri Sidhnath Prasad & Ors on 6 January, 2012
                   IN THE HIGH COURT OF JUDICATURE AT PATNA
                     Civil Writ Jurisdiction Case No.254 of 2012
                                   Ram Swaroop Mahto
                                          Versus
                               Sri Sidhnath Prasad & Ors
                             ----------------------------------

2. 06.01.2012 Heard the learned counsel for the petitioner.

This application under Article 227 of the Constitution of India has been filed by the petitioner seeking a simple direction to be issued to the Subordinate Judge, 5th, Patna City, Patna to comply the order dated 10.2.2005 passed in Civil Revision No. 72 of 2005 by this Court and to dispose of the Title Suit No. 160 of 1996 as per direction by order dated 18.7.2003 passed in Civil Revision No. 746 of 2003.

It appears that those directions were passed by this Court in Civil Revision Jurisdiction. Thus, there are already directions of this Court. There is no requirement to pass repeated orders.

In the case of M/s. M.M.T.C. Ltd. vs. Commissioner of Commercial Taxes 2009 AIR SCW 767 the Apex Court has held that under Article 226 an order, direction or writ is to issue to a person, authority or the State. In a proceeding under that Article the person, authority or State against whom the direction, order or writ is sought is a necessary party. Under Article 227 however, what comes of before the High Court is the order or judgment of a subordinate court or tribunal for the 2 purpose of ascertaining whether in giving such judgment or order that subordinate court or tribunal has acted within its authority and according to law. Admittedly, in present application under Article 227 of the constitution of India the petitioner is not challenging any order passed by the court below. It is well settled principles of law that the High Court exercises administrative supervision as well as judicial supervision. When no order passed by the court below is produced before this Court, in the application under Article 227 of the Constitution of India this court has no jurisdiction to pass any judicial order and, therefore, judicial supervision cannot be exercised under Article 227 of the Constitution of India. Thus, this writ application is not maintainable for the relief claimed by the petitioner.

Accordingly, this writ application is dismissed. 

S.S.                                  ( Mungeshwar Sahoo, J. )
