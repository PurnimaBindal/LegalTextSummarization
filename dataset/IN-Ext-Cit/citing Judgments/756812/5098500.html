Madhya Pradesh High Court
Devendra vs The State Of Madhya Pradesh on 24 October, 2017
                   M.Cr.C. No.9437 of 2017
24/10/2017

Shri   A.   S.   Kutumbale,   learned   Senior   Counsel   with Shri   Vishal   Pawar,   learned   Counsel   for   the   applicant-- Devendra S/o Late Shri Hemraj Parmar.

Shri R. R. Maheshwari, learned Public Prosecutor for the non­applicant/State.

Heard. Perused the case diary.

Applicant--Devendra,   is   husband   of   the   deceased-- Pinky. He is implicated in Crime No.281/2017 registered at Police Station, Shajapur, District Shajapur (MP) for offence punishable   under   Sections   304­B/34   of   the   Indian   Penal Code, 1860. The applicant is in custody since 22.06.2017.

Learned Senior Counsel for the applicant has drawn my attention to the decision of Apex Court in the case of Appasaheb   and   another  vs.  State   of   Maharashtra reported in 2007 (1) M.P.H.T. 209 (Supreme Court) and submitted that as per police statement of Bherulal father of the   deceased,   a   demand   for   money   on   account   of   some financial stringency has been made from the parents of the deceased and, therefore, the case in question does not come within the purview of Section 304­B of IPC. His contention is that since an essential ingredients of Section 304­B, IPC, viz., demand for dowry is not established and all other co­ accused persons have been enlarged on bail and prays for grant of bail.

Learned Public Prosecutor opposed the prayer and has drawn my attention to the police statement of Bherulal and Bagdi Bai father and mother of the deceased and prays for rejection of bail application.

On due consideration of the nature of allegation made against the present applicant and the law laid down by the Apex   Court   in   para­9,   in   the   case   of  Appasaheb   and another  vs.  State   of   Maharashtra  (supra)   so   also   the material   evidence   available   in   the   case­diary,   without expressing   any   opinion   on   the   merits   of   the   case, Miscellaneous Criminal Case No.9437/2017 is allowed. It is directed   that   the   applicant--Devendra   S/o   late   Shri Hemraj   Parmar  shall   be   released   on   bail   subject   to   his furnishing   a   personal   bond   in   the   sum   of  Rs.40,000/­ (Rupees forty thousand only) with one solvent surety in the   like   amount   to   the   satisfaction   of   the   concerned JMFC/CJM for his appearance before him or trial Court on all dates of hearing as may be fixed in this behalf by the Court concerned during trial.

C. c. as per rules.

      (P. K. Jaiswal)       Judge     pp/
