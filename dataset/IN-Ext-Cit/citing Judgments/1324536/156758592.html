1. The present appeal has been preferred by the Appellant questioning the legality of order dated 21.11.2014( herein after referred as "impugned order") , passed by Ld Traffic Court in the matter titled as State vs Satish pertaining to traffic challan number Crl. Appeal No 1 /15 Satish versus State (Page 1 of 6 ) CA No. 1/15 D.O. D 21.01.2015 Satish vs State 2828-00790-14 u/s 128/177, 129/177, 185, 39/192 and 99(1)/177 of MV Act , whereby the present appellant was convicted for the aforesaid offences and sentenced as mentioned in the impugned order.

2. Brief facts are that :-

a) On 19.11.2014, accused Satish was found driving a two wheeler bearing no.HR 10J-0716 on GTK Road, NH1 at Singhu Border . Accused was found the vehicle without rear number plate, without any valid PUC Certificate , without helmet(pillon rider) and triple riding.
b) It was alleged that as per the test conducted by the breath analyser , the quantity of alcohol was 863.4 mg per 100 ml which is very high and more than the permissible limit as provided u/s 185 M.V Act.
c) The aforesaid challan came before ld Traffic Court situated at Rohini District Court. All the accusations were explained to the appellant to which he pleaded guilty. Vide impugned order , appellant was sentenced 30 days simple imprisonment and to pay fine of Rs 4300/- u/s 128/177,129/177,185,39/192 and 99(1) /177 of MV Act .

3. Appellant has challenged the aforesaid Crl. Appeal No 1 /15 Satish versus State (Page 2 of 6 ) CA No. 1/15 D.O. D 21.01.2015 Satish vs State impugned order before this court by way of present appeal.

4. During the argument ld counsel for the appellant submitted that appellant is not assailing the conviction and he is making a request to modify the sentence on humanitarian ground. It is stated that appellant is 22 years old and is working with LIC . Ld counsel for the appellant/accused pointed out that in case appellant/accused is sent behind the bar, he will be thrown out from his job and his entire career will be ruined. Appellant/accused submit that he is the only son of his parents and there would be nobody to look after them in his absence. He further submitted that he is first time offender and he undertakes that he will not repeat such an offence again in future.

5. Ld Adll PP(Subs) for the state submitted that in the given facts and circumstances, appropriate sentence may be awarded.

6. I have perused the record and heard the ld counsel for the appellant and the ld Adll PP (subs) for the state. I have also perused the TCR.

Crl. Appeal No 1 /15 Satish versus State (Page 3 of 6 ) CA No. 1/15 D.O. D 21.01.2015 Satish vs State

7. From the record, it is evident that accused has pleaded guilty to the accusation explained to him by the ld Trial Court. It is also evident that appellant was also told about the consequence of his pleading guilty.After observing that the plea of the appellant is without any coercion, it was accepted and appellant was convicted for the offences indicated and sentenced as stated herein above.

8. Conviction has not been challanged. Even otherwise also, appellant/accused has been convicted on his plea, therefore, in these circumstances, the conviction u/s 128/177,129/177,185 , 39/192 , and 99(1) 177 of MV Act of the appellant is confirmed .

9. The main challenge is to the sentence awarded by the ld Trial Court particularly simple imprisonment for 30 days under above mentioned sections.

10. Record would indicate that although , appellant/accused has been sentenced to undergo 30 days simple imprisonment and to pay a fine of Rs 4300/- but it is not clear that how much fine and sentence has Crl. That being so, taking into consideration the submissions made on behalf of appellant/accused and the overall facts and circumstances of the present case, appellant/accused is sentenced as follows:

11. Further, section 20 of M.V Act gives power to the court to disqualify a person convicted of offence under the Act from holding any driving license to drive vehicle for such period which may be specified by the court. Clause 2 of section 20 has a reference that if a person is convicted for an offence u/s 185 M.V Act , the court " shall" order the such disqualification and which shall be for a period of not less then six months. Meaning thereby, if offender is convicted u/s 185 M.V Act , then, the court has no discretion not to disqualify the convict from holding any driving licence for a period of less then six months.

12. In the present case, it is not in dispute that the Crl. Appeal No 1 /15 Satish versus State (Page 5 of 6 ) CA No. 1/15 D.O. D 21.01.2015 Satish vs State appellant/accused has been convicted for the offence u/s 185 M.V Act also . That being so, appellant/accused is disqualified from holding any driving license to drive any class of vehicle for a period of six months from the date of this order. Intimation be sent to the concerned MLO.
