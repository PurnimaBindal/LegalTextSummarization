1. The petitioner before this Court has filed the present petition stating that the petitioners are entitled for admission in post basic nursing courses, which is being conducted at Government Nursing College, Indore.

2. The petitioners stated that the advertisement was issued on 11.12.2018 and it was stated in the advertisement that the female candidates will be given preference and the male candidates will also be permitted to participate in the process of selection. However, only girls have been given admission in the college in question.

3. The learned counsel for the petitioners argued before this court that the reservation given by the State Government is amounting to 100% reservation to the female candidates, and therefore, the same is violative of the constitutional right guaranteed to the petitioners under the Constitution of India.

4. A detailed and exhaustive reply has been filed in the matter and it has been stated on affidavit that the college in question is a girls college and the courses are being run for girls students only.

5. It has also been stated that the question of granting admission to male students in the girls college does not arise, and therefore, the respondents have not granted admission to the petitioners.

6. This Court has carefully gone through the writ petition as well as reply filed by the respondents and it is undisputed fact that the college in question where the course has started is a 100% girls college.

7. Learned Additional Advocate General has placed reply upon the judgment of Hon'ble Supreme Court in the case of Satish Menon HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH BENCH AT INDORE WP No.586/2019 (Surendra and Others Vs. State of MP and others ) 2 (Dr.) Vs. State of MP and others reported in 2009 (3) MPLJ 322 and para no.10 of the aforesaid judgment reads thus:

"10. It is therefore, clear that to enable women of this country, who for centuries were socially and economically handicapped to participate in the socio-economic activities on equal footing with men and to eliminate the backwardness of women, Article 15(3) provides that the State can make special provision for women. Such special provisions for women include reservation of seats in educational institutions or of posts in public employment in favour of women. According to Mr. Sanghi, however, no provision for reservation can be made of a single seat in an educational institution or a single post in public* employment in favour of women as per the law laid down in M.R. Balaji v. Stattf of Mysore, Deepak Sibal v. Punjab University, Dr. ChakradharPaswan v. State of Bihar and Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research}, Chandigarh v. Faculty Association and Ors. (supra). But it has been held in Toguru Sudhakar Reddy v. The Government of Andhra Pradesh (supra) that the decision of the Supreme Court in M.R. Balaji v. State of Mysore (supra), that the total reservation of seats in educational institutions under Article 15(4) should not go beyond 50% was confined only reservation under Article 15(4) of the Constitution. In Vijay Lakshmi v. Punjab University : AIR 2003 SC 3331, the Supreme Court relying upon Toguru Sudhakar Reddy v. The Government of Andhra Pradesh (supra), has observed:
Further, this Court in Toguru Sudhakar Reddy v. Government of A.P. and Ors. : 1993 Supp. (4) SCC 439, approved the reasoning of the High Court of Andhra Pradesh, wherein it was held that reservation beyond the Constitution and that ratio in M.R. Halaji v. State of Mysore, 1963 Supp. (1) SCR 439, was only confined to the reservation under ArticleS 15(4) and 16(4) of the Constitution of India.
Hence, the decisions of the Supreme Court under Articles 15(4) and 16(4) that reservations in favour of SC, ST and OBC categories cannot exceed 50% or cannot be 100% do not apply to a special provision in favour of women under Article 15(3) of the Constitution"

8. In light of the aforesaid judgment, as the State by way of policy HIGH COURT OF MADHYA PRADESH BENCH AT INDORE WP No.586/2019 (Surendra and Others Vs. State of MP and others ) 3 decision has started a 100% girls college and no other male candidate has been admitted since inception, the question of granting admission to the petitioners does not arise.

9. It is not a case of 100% reservation to the female candidates.

10. Accordingly, the admission is declined.

11. Petition is dismissed.
