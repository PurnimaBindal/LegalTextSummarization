                      J U D G M E N T

Thottathil B.Radhakrishnan, J.

We have heard learned counsel for the appellant and learned Senior Government Pleader for the Department of Commercial Taxes.

2. Under challenge is the judgment, whereby the learned single Judge refused to interfere with the condition imposed by the first appellate authority in an appeal under the KVAT Act. That authority ordered payment of 30% of the outstanding demand to sustain the order of stay granted. The petitioner/writ appellant contends that no amount should have been imposed for deposit as a condition for stay.

3. The petitioner is a dealer in bakery and confectionery products. There are two components in the assessment order. One is for payment of tax at the rate of W.A.No.460 of 2016 2 12.5%, on the basis that the dealer has a registered trade mark. The second is denial of 'input tax credit'.

4. We sustain the impugned order of the appellate authority for payment of 30% to the extent it relates to 30% of input tax credit component of the appeal pending before the appellate authority.

5. Regarding the issue of payment of tax at 12.5%, on the ground that the dealer has a registered trade mark, Ext.P1 certificate shows that the trade mark application was on 26.6.2009; but it was issued by the Registrar of Trade Marks, only on 27.7.2013. The provisions of the Trade Marks Act provide a deeming provision whereby the effect of registration certificate is made applicable from a retrospective date. It is trite law that such provision is based on the principle of deemed equivalence to public user of such mark, as held by the Honourable Supreme Court in Meghraj Biscuits Industries Ltd. v. Commissioner of Central Excise, U.P. [2007 (210) W.A.No.460 of 2016 3 ELT 161 (SC)] and followed by Their Lordships in Commissioner of Central Excise, Bangalore v. M/S.Meyer Health Care (Pvt.) Ltd. & Ors. (Civil Appeal Nos.4052-4054 of 2003, judgment dated 24.7.2011). With the availability of these judgments, we are unable to insist on the appellant as a dealer paying the 30% component of the demand based on the rate of tax being 12.5% on the goods. That portion of the impugned order of the first appellate authority is liable to be quashed.

In the result, this writ appeal is allowed, vacating the impugned judgment and modifying Ext.P4 order, and ordering that stay of collection of the disputed amount for the year 2011-12 will be subject to the condition that the appellant pays 30% of the amount in dispute under the head "Input Tax Credit" and furnishes adequate security for the balance amount of the input tax credit component before the assessing authority within a period of three W.A.No.460 of 2016 4 weeks from today. Ext.P4 will stand modified accordingly. If any of the conditions of this judgment, as stated above, is not fulfilled, the effect of Ext.P4 order will stand vacated.
