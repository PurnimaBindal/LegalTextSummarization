JUDGMENT:

1. This revision petition under Section 397 CrPC is directed against the order dated 05.08.2017 passed by the Ld. MM (Mahila Court­04) in State case arising out of FIR No. 753/07 under Section 498A/406/34 IPC registered at P.S. Rajouri Garden whereby charge for the offence punishable under Section 498A IPC was directed to be framed against the revisionist.   

Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State                                     Page No.  1/ 8    
                                            Crl. Revision No. 413/2017

2. The brief background relevant for the disposal of the revision petition is that the above mentioned FIR was registered against the revisionist and his relatives on the basis of the complaint dated 17.07.2007 lodged by the complainant Smt. Sonia Gupta. The revisionist herein is the husband of the complainant. After the completion of investigation, chargesheet was filed against the revisionist and ten of his relatives on 16.09.2009. Vide order dated 07.12.2009, only the revisionist was summoned by the trial court.

3. Though the revisionist was discharged of the offence under Section 406 IPC but prima facie sufficient material was found for framing charge for the offence under Section 498A IPC against him by the trial court. Aggrieved with the said order on charge, the revisionist has approached this court. 

4. The order has been assailed by the counsel for revisionist by contending that the trial court has failed to appreciate that the complaint had been lodged by the complainant as a counter blast to the divorce petition instituted by the revisionist on 02.05.2007 so as to pressurize him to withdraw the same and that the complainant has failed to provide any medical record in Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State   Page No.  2/ 8     Crl. Revision No. 413/2017 support of her allegations of physical abuse at the hands of the revisionist on 04.04.2007. He argued that the trial court also erred in observing that the allegations in the complaint are sufficient to attract Section 498A IPC and failed to appreciate that the alleged demand of money by the revisionist from the complainant and her brothers after the marriage for the purpose of business or to meet the financial stringency can not be said to be demand of dowry. In support of his contention, the counsel for the revisionist placed reliance on Dhula Ram v. State of Rajasthan, 1995 Crl. L.J 4057 and Appasaheb v. State of Maharashtra, 2007 AIR (SC) 763. Based on the above submissions, he urged that the impugned order be set aside and the revisionist be discharged of the offence under Section 498A IPC. 

5. On the other hand, Ld. APP for State submitted that there is no infirmity or illegality in the impugned order and therefore, no interference is called for.  

6. The contention of the counsel for the revisionist that the alleged demand of money by the revisionist from the complainant and her brothers after the marriage for the purpose of business can Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State   Page No.  3/ 8     Crl. Revision No. 413/2017 not be said to be demand of dowry so as to attract Section 498A IPC is misconceived. In both the judgments i.e. Dhula Ram v. State of Rajasthan, 1995 Crl. L.J 4057 and Appasaheb v. State of Maharashtra, 2007 AIR (SC) 763 relied upon by the counsel for the revisionist, the Hon'ble Courts were dealing with the appeals against conviction under Section 304B IPC and after referring to the definition of 'dowry ' as provided under Section 2 of Dowry Prohibition Act, it had been held that a demand for money on account of some financial stringency or for meeting some urgent domestic expense can not be termed as demand for dowry and that in order to fall within the ambit of term 'dowry', the demand of money must have some co­relation with the marriage of the parties. While under Section 304B IPC, which prescribes punishment for dowry death, the cruelty or harassment of the woman is for, or in connection with, any demand for 'dowry', Section 498A is of wider amplitude and encompasses all sorts of cruelties or harassments caused to the woman to meet any unlawful demand for property or valuable security irrespective of whether the same is in connection with marriage or otherwise. For the sake of convenience, Section 498A IPC, which prescribes punishment for subjecting a women to cruelty by her husband or relatives of husband, is reproduced Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State   Page No.  4/ 8     Crl. Revision No. 413/2017 as under:­  498A. Husband or relative of husband of a woman subjecting her to cruelty:­ Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years and shall also be liable to fine,  Explanation:­ For the purpose of this section, "cruelty" means­

(a)  any willful conduct which is of such a nature as is likely to drive the woman to commit suicide or to cause grave injury or danger to life, limb or health (whether mental or physical) of the woman; or

(b) harassment of the woman where such harassment is with a view to coercing her or any person related to her to meet any unlawful demand for any property or valuable security or is on account of failure by her or any person related to her to meet such demand. 

Hence, the judgments relied upon by the counsel for the revisionist are not applicable to the facts of the present case and do not render any assistance to him. 

7 At this juncture, it would be important to refer to the guidelines laid down by the Superior Courts to be kept in mind at the stage of framing of charge. In Union of India v. Prafulla Kumar Samal, AIR 1979 SC 366, the Hon'ble Apex Court laid down the following principles:  

Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State                                   Page No.  5/ 8    
                                              Crl. Revision No. 413/2017

(i) That the Judge while considering the question of framing the charges under Section 227 of the Code has the undoubted power to sift and weigh the evidence for the limited purpose of  finding out whether or not a prima facie case against the accused has been made out;

(ii) Where the materials placed before the Court disclose grave suspicion against the accused which has not been properly explained the Court will be fully justified in framing a charge and proceeding with the trial;

(iii) The test to determine a prima facie case would naturally depend upon the facts of each case and it is difficult to lay down a rule of universal application. By and large however if two views are equally possible and the Judge is satisfied that the evidence produced before him while giving rise to some suspicion but not grave suspicion against the accused, he will be fully within his right to discharge the accused; and 

(iv) That in exercising his jurisdiction under Section 227 of the Code the Judge which under the present Code is a senior and experienced Court can not act merely as a Post­Office or a mouth­piece of the prosecution, but has to consider the broad probabilities of the case, the total effect of the evidence and the documents produced before the Court, any basic infirmities appearing in the case and so on. This however does not mean that the Judge should make a roving enquiry into the pros and cons of the matter and weigh the evidence as if he was conducting a trial. 

8 Upon testing the First Information Report, statements recorded under Section 161 CrPC and other material on record on the Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State   Page No.  6/ 8     Crl. Revision No. 413/2017 touchstone of the above principles, there can be no doubt that there exist the grounds to presume that the revisionist has committed an offence under Section 498A Indian Penal Code. At this stage, the case of the prosecution can not be thrown out by disbelieving the averments or the allegations in the complaint lodged by the complainant Smt. Sonia Gupta merely because the revisionist had filed the divorce petition against her prior to the lodging of the complaint. From a bare reading of the FIR, it is seen that there are clear and specific accusations against the revisionist in as much as the complainant has alleged that since the date of marriage on 16.05.1991, the revisionist alongwith his family members started harassing her, physically as well as mentally, for demand of dowry; that the revisionist and his relatives were under the impression that her brothers were financially affluent and therefore, they used to pressurise her to bring money from her brothers as financial help for their business; that whenever she used to refuse to bring the money, the revisionist and his family members used to give beatings to her; that on 09.11.2003, she came to know from the family members of the revisionist that he was having illicit relations with some other woman; that compelled by the behaviour of the revisionist, father and brothers of the complainant provided financial assistance to the revisionist on various occasions; that on 04.04.2007, when the Narender Kumar Gupta  v. State   Page No.  7/ 8     Crl. Revision No. 413/2017 complainant returned home after attending a function at her parental home, the revisionist gave beatings to her with a leather belt resulting in blue injury marks on her body; that on 10.04.2007, the revisionist contacted the father of the complainant and made demand of money; that on 25.04.2007, when the complainant gave a call to the revisionist and requested him to take her back to the matrimonial home, he refused by stating that she would not be allowed to return until and unless she brought the money; that on 30.04.2007, when the complainant alongwith the children came to the matrimonial home on her own, the revisionist did not allow her to enter the house and called the police. In view of the above specific allegations, the trial court rightly formed the opinion that prima facie case was made out for framing charge for the commission of offence under Section 498A IPC against the revisionist.    

9 For the forgoing reasons, I find no infirmity or illegality in the order dated 05.08.2017 passed by the Ld. MM. The revision petition is without merits and is accordingly dismissed.   File of revision petition be consigned to record room.
