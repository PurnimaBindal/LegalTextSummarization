39.Before appreciating the evidence which has been led on record by prosecution in support of charges, I would like to discuss here the law with regard to offence of Section 304B IPC.

40.In order to attract Section 304B IPC, prosecution is required to prove the following ingredients:--

(i) The death of a woman should be caused by burns or bodily injury or otherwise than under a normal circumstance.

(ii) Such a death should have occurred within seven years of her marriage.

(iii) She must have been subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or any relative of her husband.

(iv) Such cruelty or harassment should be for or in connection with demand of dowry.

(v) Such cruelty or harassment is shown to have been meted out to the woman soon before her death.

41.For appreciating section 304B IPC, presumption u/s 113(b) of Indian Evidence Act is also relevant. Section 113(b) of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 provides that when the question is whether a person has committed dowry death of a woman or not then if it is shown that soon before her death such woman had been subjected to cruelty or harassment for, or in connection with, any demand for dowry, the Court shall presume that such person had caused the dowry death. 

FIR NO: 191/09                                                    16/41
                                               State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

42.Therefore, presumption u/s 113(b) of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 can only be raised when proof of women being subjected to cruelty or harassment for the purpose of dowry soon before her death has been established by prosecution on record.

43.On conjoint reading of Section 304B IPC and Section 113(b) of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, the expression "soon before" is very relevant. Where any act of cruelty or harassment for the purpose of dowry had taken place soon before death of deceased or not, will depend upon the facts and circumstances of each and every case and the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India has laid down the criteria for application of "soon before" expression as laid down in Section 113(b) of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872.

44.In the matter of M.Srinivasulu Vs. State of AP, AIR 2007 (SC) 1436, the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India has discussed the applicability of expression "soon before" as under in para 10:--

"A conjoint reading of Section 113B of the Evidence Act and Section 304B IPC shows that there must be material to show that soon before her death the victim was subjected to cruelty or harassment. Prosecution has to rule out the possibility of a natural or accidental death so as to bring it within the purview of the `death occurring otherwise than in normal circumstances'. The FIR NO: 191/09 17/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 expression `soon before' is very relevant where Section 113B of the Evidence Act and Section 304B IPC are pressed into service. Prosecution is obliged to show that soon before the occurrence there was cruelty or harassment and only in that case presumption operates. Evidence in that regard has to be led by prosecution. `Soon before' is a relative term and it would depend upon circumstances of each case and no strait-jacket formula can be laid down as to what would constitute a period soon before the occurrence. It would be hazardous to indicate any fixed period, and that brings in the importance of a proximity test both for the proof of an offence of dowry death as well as for raising a presumption under Section 113B of the Evidence Act. The expression `soon before her death' used in the substantive Section 304B IPC and Section 113B of the Evidence Act is present with the idea of proximity test. No definite period has been indicated and the expression `soon before' is not defined. A reference to expression `soon after' used in Section 114 (illustration (a)) of the Evidence Act is relevant. It lays down that a Court may presume that a man who is in the possession of goods soon after the theft, is either the thief or has received the goods knowing them to be stolen, unless he can account for his possession. The determination of the period which can come within the term `soon before' is left to be determined by the Courts, depending upon facts and circumstances of each case. Suffice, However, to indicate that the expression `soon before' normally imply that the interval should not be much between the concerned cruelty or harassment and the death in question. There muse be existence of a proximate and live-link between the effect of cruelty based on dowry demand and the concerned death. If FIR NO: 191/09 18/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 alleged incident of cruelty is remote in time and has become stale enough not to disturb mental equilibrium of the woman concerned, it would be of no consequence."

45.In the light of law discussed herein above, the onus is upon prosecution to show that soon before death of deceased Babita, she was subjected to cruelty and harassment for the purpose of dowry.

52.Although from the suicide note Ex.PW18/A, it is not known as to what prompted deceased to commit suicide but one thing is definitely known from suicide note Ex.PW18/A that she was not harassed or tortured by her husband and his relatives FIR NO: 191/09 21/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 i.e.accused persons for the purpose of dowry. There is no allegation made by deceased in her suicide note Ex.PW18/A that due to torture and harassment by accused persons for the purpose of dowry, she was committing suicide. The suicide note Ex.PW18/A can be equated with the dying declaration of the deceased. It is a settled principle of law that lot of sanctity is attached to a dying declaration as a person while dying speaks truth and that the deceased was speaking truth through her suicide note Ex.PW18/A is borne from the fact that in the suicide note Ex.PW18/A, deceased had herself mentioned that while dying, she will not tell lies. Therefore, suicide note Ex.PW18/A of deceased shows that deceased had pen down her honest feelings regarding accused persons. There is not an iota of allegation against any of accused made by deceased in her suicide note Ex.PW18/A that she was harassed or tortured for the purpose of dowry due to which she was committing suicide. Therefore, on the basis of suicide note Ex.PW18/A, it is not shown by prosecution that deceased was subjected to cruelty for the purpose of dowry soon before her death.

53.It was contended by counsel for complainant during arguments that much reliance upon suicide note Ex.PW18/A cannot be placed as it is undated and it is quite possible that suicide note Ex.PW18/A might have been written long time back and, FIR NO: 191/09 22/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

85.The facts of the present case are squarely covered by the judgment of Hon'ble Supreme Court of India delivered in the matter of Arulvelu & another's case (supra). The facts before the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India were that deceased had died within six years of marriage. She had left behind a suicide note where she had stated that nobody is responsible for her death. The trial court after relying upon the suicide note and after finding that no evidence had come on record that she was subjected to cruelty, acquitted the accused persons u/s 304- B/498A IPC.

86.In appeal, the Hon'ble High Court had reversed the finding of trial court.

87.In appeal by accused persons, the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India agreeing with the finding of ld.trial court upheld the order of the trial court and acquitted all accused persons u/s 304B and 498A IPC.

88.The aforementioned judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India is applicable to the facts and circumstances of the present case as in the present case also, suicide note of deceased has been proved on record wherein she has not held FIR NO: 191/09 37/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 anyone responsible for her death and even the evidence led by her parents, her brother and her sister do not show that she was subjected to cruelty on account of dowry soon before her death.

89.Counsel for complainant has relied upon the following judgments of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India in support of his contention that accused are liable to be convicted u/s 304B and 498A IPC:--

(1) Pathan Hussain Basha vs State Of A.P., AIR 2012 SC 3205 (2) Bansi Lal Vs. State of Haryana, AIR 2011 SC 691 (3) Nandyalavenkataramana Vs. State of Andhra Pradesh, AIR 2011 SC 567.

(4) Bachni Devi and anr. Vs. State of Haryana through Secretary, AIR 2011 SC 1098.

90.I have carefully perused the aforementioned judgments relied upon by the counsel for complainant. They are not applicable to the facts and circumstances of the present case.

91.In Pathan Hussain Basha'a case (supra), deceased had died within few months of her marriage and evidence of father of deceased and other witnesses proved that persistent dowry demands were made by accused persons and that they used to harass the deceased and used to beat her up. Therefore, FIR NO: 191/09 38/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 accused was found guilty for the offence u/s 304B IPC.

92.However, in the present case, there is no evidence led on record by relatives of deceased that she was being harassed for the purpose of dowry. Therefore, present judgment is not applicable.

93.The second judgment of the Hon'ble Supreme Court i.e.Bansi Lal's case (supra) is also not applicable to the present case as in that case, defence of appellant/accused was that deceased had left behind a suicide note. However, the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India did not believe the suicide note produced by appellant/accused as it was not proved that it was written by deceased herself and even witnesses in whose presence suicide note was recovered, were not examined. Further, demand of scooter was proved in that case soon before death of deceased.

94.However, in the present case, suicide note Ex.PW18/A is proved to have been written by deceased wherein she has not held anyone responsible for her death. Further, there is no evidence led on record that immediately prior to her death, she was tortured for dowry by accused persons. Hence, aforementioned judgment is not applicable to the facts of the present case.

95.In Nandyalavenkataramana' case (supra), the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India had dismissed the appeal filed by the husband against his conviction u/s 304B and 498A IPC. In this matter, it was proved that dying declaration made by deceased in the form of letters were in her handwriting which were held to inculpate appellant/husband for treating deceased with cruelty for dowry.

96.However, in the present case, dying declaration in the form of suicide note Ex.PW18/A does not inculpate any of accused persons for harassing deceased for the purpose of dowry. Hence, the aforementioned judgment is not applicable.

97.In the matter of Bachni Devi's case (supra) it was proved on record that soon before her death, there was demand of motorcycle by accused persons and hence, appeal of accused persons with regard to offence u/s 304B IPC was dismissed.

98.However, in the present case, no demand of dowry soon before death has been proved against accused persons. On contrary, suicide note which is in the form of dying declaration holds no one responsible for the death of deceased.

99.In the light of above discussion, prosecution has not been able FIR NO: 191/09 40/41 State Vs. Vikas and Ors.

FIR No: 191/09 to prove its case that deceased committed suicide on account of she being harassed for the purpose of dowry soon before her death. Even allegations against accused persons u/s 498A IPC that she was being treated with cruelty have not been proved in the light of suicide note Ex.PW18/A.
