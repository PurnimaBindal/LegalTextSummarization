JUDGMENT

PROSECUTION CASE

1.      Brief facts as per prosecution case are as under:­ 

(a) That on dated 13.03.2012 at about 2141 hrs, a PCR call was received in PS Kalyan Puri from W/Ct. Sunita that one lady r/o 1/259, Trilokpuri, Delhi, has committed suicide. The said information was recorded as DD No.29A (Ex.PW11/A) and it was marked to ASI Om Prakash. Thereafter, ASI   Om Prakash reached at the spot i.e. 1/259, Trilok Puri, Delhi, where he found that FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 1 of 30 the door of the room situated at 1 st floor was open and one lady was  found sitting beside double bed. The said lady was found dead and a piece of rope was found tied with her neck while remaining portion of rope was found tied with ceiling fan and its corners appeared to have been freshly cut. The SHO and   ACP   concerned   along   with   other   staff   also  reached   at   the   spot.   The name of the deceased was revealed as Smt. Mithlesh w/o Brijesh Kumar @ Pintoo. SDM Preet Vihar was informed who reached at the spot and took stock of the situation.  SDM conducted inquiry u/s 176 Cr.P.C and recorded statement of Smt. Saroj, the mother of deceased and Sarvesh, the brother of deceased. SDM forwarded the statement (Ex.PW2/A) of Smt. Saroj to SHO for necessary action. On the statement of complainant Smt. Saroj, present FIR was registered u/s 498A/304A/34 IPC and investigation was marked to ASI Om Prakash.

(b) In her statement, Smt. Saroj stated that her daughter Mithlesh got married with Brijesh on 27.11.2006. After marriage, her daughter started living with accused   Brijesh   at   1/259,   Trilokpuri,   Delhi   where   her   in­laws   were   also residing. Accused Birjesh and his parents used to demand dowry. Accused Brijesh was having illicit relation with his bhabhi Sangeeta which was being objected to by her daughter due to which her daughter was beaten several times. Accused Brijesh was jobless and he used to beat her daughter after consuming   liquor.   Deceased   had   narrated   her   about   all   these   facts. Complainant   stated   that   she   is   sure   that   accused   Brijesh,   Ram   Sahai James   (father­in­law),   Rameshwari   (mother­in­law),   brother   Dinesh   and bhabhi Sangeeta have killed her daughter.

FACT IN ISSUE

3.       Points which emerged for determination in this case are:

Whether   accused   persons   subjected   the   deceased   Mithlesh   to physical   and   mental   cruelty   and   harassment   before   her   death,   for dowry and whether the harassment caused by the accused led the deceased to commit suicide?

LEGAL POSITION

10. At   the   outset,   to   appreciate   submissions   and   before   making discussions   on   the   testimony   of   material   witnesses,   the   legal   position   in regard   to   the   provisions   of   section   498   A   &   304­B   IPC   is   required   to   be noted:­  The ingredients of Section 498­A IPC are as follows:­ "Section:­498A:   Husband   or   relative   of   husband   of   a   woman subjecting her to cruelty ­ Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman to cruelty shall be punished   with   imprisonment   for   a   term   which   may   extend   to   three years and shall also be liable to fine.

Explanation ­ For the purpose of this section 'cruelty' means­

(a) any willful conduct which is of such a nature as is likely to drive the woman to commit suicide or to cause grave injury or danger to life, limb or health (whether mental or physical) of the woman; or

(b) harassment of the woman where such harassment is with a view to coercing   her   or   any   person   related   to   her   to   meet   any   unlawful demand   for   any   property   or   valuable   security   or   is   on   account   of failure by her or any person related to her to meet such demand."

11.   Consequences of cruelty which are likely to drive a woman to commit suicide or  to  cause grave injury or danger to life, limb or health, whether mental or physical of the woman are required to be established in order to bring home the application of Section 498A IPC. Cruelty has been defined in the   Explanation   for   the   purpose   of   Section   498A.   As   per   settled   law   the provisions of Sections 304B and 498A, IPC are not mutually inclusive. These FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 15 of 30 provisions deal with two distinct offences. Cruelty is a common essential to both the Sections and that has to  be proved. The Explanation to Section 498A   gives   the   meaning   of   'cruelty'.   In   Section   304B   there   is   no   such explanation   about   the   meaning   of   'cruelty'.   But   having   regard   to   common background   to   these   offences,   meaning   of   'cruelty'   or   'harassment'   is   the same as prescribed in the Explanation to Section 498A under which 'cruelty' by itself amounts to an offence.

12.     Essential ingredients of Section 304­B IPC are as follows:­   Sec:304 B:­

(i) The death of a woman should be caused by burns or bodily injury or otherwise than under a normal circumstance.

(ii)   Such   a   death   should   have   occurred   within   seven   years   of   her marriage.

(iii)She   must   have   been   subjected   to   cruelty   or   harassment   by   her husband or any relative of her husband.

(iv)Such   cruelty   or   harassment   should   be   for   or   in   connection   with demand of dowry.

(v)Such cruelty or harassment is shown to have been meted out to the woman soon before her death.

13.      The  harassment  has to  be  in connection with  demand of  dowry.  In Appasaheb   and   another   vs.   State   of   Maharashtra,   2007   (1)   Crimes   110 Hon'ble Supreme Court observed that:­ "A demand for money on account of some financial stringency or for meeting some urgent domestic expenses or for purchasing manure cannot be termed as a demand for dowry as the said word is normally understood.   The   evidence   adduced   by   the   prosecution   does   not, therefore, show that any demand for "dowry" as defined in Section 2 of the Dowry Prohibition Act was made by the appellants as what was allegedly asked for was some money for meeting domestic expenses and for purchasing manure. Since an essential ingredient of Section FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 16 of 30 304B, I.P.C. viz. demand for dowry is not established, the conviction of the appellants cannot be sustained."

14. As per the definition of "dowry death" in Section 304­B IPC and the wording   of   Section   113­B   of   the   Evidence   Act,   one   of   the   essential ingredients,   amongst   others,   in   both   the   provisions   is   that   the   concerned woman must have been subjected to cruelty or harassment "soon before her death" and that too "for or in connection with the demand for dowry". The legal position is well settled that on a joint reading of Section 113­B of the Evidence   Act   and   Section   304­B   IPC   it   would   reveal   that   there   must   be cogent material to show that soon before her death the victim was subjected to cruelty or harassment, and that too in connection with any demand for dowry.   The   prosecution   has   to   rule   out   the   possibility   of   a   natural   or accidental death so as to bring it within the purview of the "death occurring otherwise than in normal circumstances". The expression "soon before" is very relevant where presumption u/s 113­B of the Evidence Act and Section 304­B IPC are pressed into service. In the case reported as  2014 (9) SCC 645 titled as Manohar Lal Vs State  of Haryana Hon'ble Supreme Court has   observed  that   prosecution   is   obliged   to   prove   that   soon   before   the occurrence   there   was   cruelty   or   harassment;   and   only   thereafter,   the presumption under section 113­B of Evidence Act would operate. 

15.   Evidence   in   this   regard   has   to   be   led   in   by   the   prosecution   and established beyond doubt.  In a case reported as Sunil Bajaj vs. State of M.P.   2001   CrLJ   (SC)   4700,   Hon'ble   Supreme   Court  observed   that   the allegations of dowry death have to be carefully scrutinized keeping in view of the gravity of punishment.

16. In regard to the term "Soon before" in the case of Hira Lal & Others v. State (Govt. of NCT), Delhi, (2003)8 SCC 80. the Hon'ble Supreme Court has made the  following observations:­ "The expression 'soon before' is very relevant where Section 113­B of the Evidence Act and Section 304 B Indian Penal Code are pressed into   service.   Prosecution   is   obliged   to   show   that   soon   before   the occurrence   there   was   cruelty   or   harassment   and   only   in   that   case presumption   operates.   Evidence   in   that   regard   has   to   be   led   by prosecution. 'Soon before' is a relative term and it would depend upon circumstances of each case and no strait­jacket formula can be laid down   as   to   what   would   constitute   a   period   of   soon   before   the occurrence. It would be hazardous to indicate any fixed period, and that brings in the importance of a proximity test both for the proof of an offence   of  dowry  death   as well   as  for  raising   a   presumption   under Section 113­B of the Evidence Act. The expression 'soon before her death' used in the substantive Section 304 B Indian Penal Code and Section 113­B of the Evidence Act is present with the idea of proximity test. No definite period has been indicated and the expression 'soon before' is not defined. A reference to expression 'soon before' used in Section   114,   Illustration   (a)   of   the   Evidence   Act   is   relevant.   It   lays down that a Court may presume that a man who is in the possession of goods 'soon after the theft, is either the thief has received the goods knowing them to be stolen, unless he can account for his possession. The determination of the period which can come within the term 'soon before' is left to be determined by the Courts, depending upon facts and circumstances of each case. Suffice, however, to indicate that the expression 'soon before' would normally imply that the interval should not be much between the concerned cruelty or harassment and the death in question. There must be existence of a proximate and live­ link between the effect of cruelty based on dowry demand and the concerned death. If alleged incident of cruelty is remote in time and has   become   stale   enough   not   to   disturb   mental   equilibrium   of   the woman concerned, it would be of no consequence."

17.      In the case of Vipin Jaiswal Vs State of A.P. 2013(3) SCC 684 the Hon'ble Supreme Court has observed that the allegations of cruelty have to be specific. The relevant para of this judgment reads as under:­  FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 18 of 30 "In any case, to hold an accused guilty of both the offences under Sections 304B and  498A,  Indian  Penal   Code,  the  prosecution  is required to prove beyond reasonable doubt that the deceased was subjected   to   cruelty   or   harassment   by   the   accused.   From   the evidence of the prosecution witnesses, and in particular PW1 and PW4,   we   find   that   they   have   made   general   allegations   of harassment by the appellant towards the deceased and have not brought in evidence any specific acts of cruelty or harassment by the appellant on the deceased."

18.   Hon'ble Supreme Court has taken the same view in the case of 2014 (9) SCC 645 titled as Manohar Lal Vs State of Haryana Hon'ble Supreme Court. 

19.  In the recent case reported as Monju Roy & ors. Vs State of West Bengal   2015(3)   Recent   Apex   Judgments   (R.A.J.)   472  the   Hon'ble Supreme Court has observed that for proving the offence under section 304­ B the harassment has to be proved alongwith demand. It is also held that prosecution is to also establish that all family members caused harassment.

20. In the light of above settled proposition of law, facts of the case in hand will  be  analyzed   to  ascertain   as  to   whether   prosecution   has   led  sufficient evidence   to   bring   home   the   guilt   of   accused   persons   for   the   offence punishable under Section 304B IPC or not.

her first statement, she did not raise any allegation that prior to the marriage of the deceased, accused Brijesh had demanded any dowry, however, in her evidence, she stated that prior to marriage accused Brijesh had demanded a four wheeler, however, somehow they had given a Pulsar motorcycle to the accused at the time of marriage. The complainant had not specified, in her statement Ex.PW2/A, the demand and also not stated if the deceased was ever harassed by the accused, on that account, however, in her evidence, she stated that accused persons used to demand 2.5 lacs rupees and also gave beatings to her. She also added that accused Brijesh used to beat her daughter   and   demand   cash   and   dowry.   She   also   stated   that   once   the accused persons had tried to strangulate her daughter. She also stated in her testimony, though did not state earlier, that on the occasion of birth of twins, FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 22 of 30 they had given Rs.5000/­ cash and clothes in Chhochhak, however, accused persons thrown away the cash and clothes. During cross­examination, she also brought  one altogether new allegation that one day prior to the incident, her   daughter   was   beaten   by   the   accused   Brijesh   on   account   of   dowry demand. During cross­examination, PW2 stated that she could not tell all the facts to the SDM as at that time she was hopeless. The reason put­forth by her   for   not   mentioning   the   allegations   which   she     mentioned   in   her examination as well as in her cross­examination to the SDM, is not reliable as   it   is   clear   from   her   statement   that   she   had   stated   in   her   statement Ex.PW2/A   that   she   had   given   this   statement   in   her   full   senses   and   the statement given by her to the SDM was read over to her. Thus, it is clear that the complainant was well aware about the contents of her first statement. Furthermore, even if it is assumed that she was hopeless at the time of giving her   first   statement   she   ought   to   have   made   an   application   for   giving supplementary   statement   if   something   was   missed   by   her   at   the   time   of giving   her   first   statement.   However,   complainant   did   not   make   any application   to   the   SDM   concerned   for   expressing   her   willingness   to   give further statement. Furthermore, the allegations leveled by her in her evidence are either contradictory to her previous statement and are also bereft of the necessary details. She has stated that once the accused persons had tried to strangulate his daughter, however, she has not given any particulars as to on which date and time the said incident had occurred. Likewise, she has also stated that  one day prior  to the incident, accused Brijesh had  beaten the deceased for dowry, however, her this specific allegation is not trustworthy as firstly she neither stated this same before the SDM nor in her examination in FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 23 of 30 chief   and   during   her   cross­examination,   she   voluntarily   stated   the   same. Furthermore, complainant has not mentioned as to how she came to know that the deceased was beaten up by the accused Brijesh a day prior to the incident. These particular allegations are not trustworthy for another reason as these allegations are not supported by medical evidence. It is clear from the autopsy report that the deceased had only suffered the injuries which were due to hanging and there were no other external injury marks on her body. Besides, in relation to the allegations of demand of Rs.2.5 lacs, she has given a contradictory statement. As per examination in chief of PW2 the demand of cash was made after about 4­5 months of the marriage, however, as per cross­examination for the first time, the demand of Rs.2.5 lacs cash was made at the time of first delivery of the deceased. 

28.  Besides, there are several material contradictions in the testimony of all   three   material   witnesses   in   regard   to   the   nature   of   demand,   time   of demand,   purpose   of   demand   as   well   as   nature   of   harassment   allegedly caused by the accused persons to the deceased. It is clear from the perusal of   the   statements   given   by   PW2   and   PW3   to   the   SDM   that   they   did   not mention as to what kind of demand used to be made by the accused and why such a demand was being made. However, during their evidence, all three material witnesses stated that the accused persons used to demand Rs.2.5 lacs, however,  all these witnesses have given  a different version regarding the time as well as purpose of the demand. PW2 stated that the said demand was firstly made at the time of first delivery of Mithlesh while PW3 stated that after about 2­3 weeks of the marriage, the said demand was made. PW4 stated   that   the   said   demand   was   made   sometimes   after   Chhochhak FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 24 of 30 ceremony. As far as the purpose of demand is concerned, PW2 stated that accused were demanding the said amount on the pretext that deceased has not brought anything from her parental house. In this regard, PW3 has given a different statement regarding the nature of demand as well as its purpose. He stated that accused used to demand Rs.2.5 to 3 lacs cash along with a four wheeler as accused Brijesh wanted to start his business. Thus, it is clear that firstly  the  material  witnesses  did not  specify  the demand  in their first statements and they only specified the same during their evidence and while stating the same  they  have  contradicted  each  other regarding the  nature, time and purpose of demand.

29.   PW2, in her statement to the SDM, stated that the accused Brijesh was jobless and used to beat deceased after consuming liquor. PW3 stated in her evidence that accused Brijesh was not having any business and was unable   to   maintain   his   four   children.   These   allegations   are   also   not trustworthy as it is clear from cross­examination of all these witnesses that they admitted that accused Brijesh was running a mobile repairing shop and Sandeep   @   Deepu   (brother   of   deceased)   had   stayed   in   the   matrimonial house of the deceased for 2 and ½ months to learn mobile repair work. Thus, it is clear from this part of the cross­examination that the accused Brijesh was not   jobless   and   he   was   running   mobile   repairing   shop   for   earning   his livelihood and he has also imparted training to his brother­in­law regarding mobile   repair   work   and   later   on   Deepu   had   opened   a   shop   for   mobile repairing work. Thus, the allegations that the accused Brijesh was jobless and used to beat the deceased after consuming liquor also not trustworthy. It is admitted position of fact that the son of complainant had stayed in the FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 25 of 30 matrimonial house of the deceased for about 2 and 1/s months period and in case the accused Brijesh or other accused persons would have harassed the deceased,  he would have brought  these facts into  the notice of his other family members i.e. PW2 to PW4 and they would have stated these facts in their statements to the SDM as well as during their evidence. Furthermore, it is also admitted position of fact that Sandeep @ Deepu had stated in the matrimonial house of deceased in the year 2008 and the accused Brijesh had trained him in mobile repair work. In case, the accused Brijesh and other accused   persons   would   be   hostile   to   the   deceased   and   accused   Brijesh would be a person of immoral character, neither the complainant would have sent her son to the matrimonial house of a daughter nor accused would have agreed to keep him in his house for a considerable period of 2 and ½ months to impart training to him.   These facts also show that in the year 2008, the complainant and accused persons were maintaining cordial relations, thus, under   these   circumstances   the   allegations   that   the   accused   had   been demanding dowry and harassing the deceased from the period sometimes after the marriage, are not trustworthy on this count also. Besides the reason putforth by the material witnesses behind demand of cash and four wheeler also not trustworthy as according to PW3 the demand of cash of Rs.2.5 lacs and   a   four   wheeler   was   made   as   accused   Brijesh   wanted   to   start   his business. While it is clear that he was already running a shop and it is also clear   from   cross­examination   of   PW2   that   the   accused   Brijesh   was   also maintaining   an   Esteem   car.   Furthermore,   it   is   well   settled   law   that   the demand of cash or a vehicle for running a business cannot be termed to be a dowry demand.

FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 26 of 30

30. The complainant has alleged that the accused Brijesh was having illicit relation with his bhabhi Sangeeta and due to this reason accused Brijesh used to beat the deceased. PW3 also stated that accused Brijesh was having illicit relation with his sister­in­law (bhabhi) as well as with another woman and he was not happy with this marriage and wanted to get rid of deceased. Thus,   both   the  said   witnesses   also  endeavoured   to  say   that   the   accused Brijesh   used   to   harass   the   deceased   on   this   count   also,   however,   these allegations are also not reliable during cross­examination of PW2 and PW3, the accused wanted to contend that accused Sangeeta and Dinesh used to reside   at   Ghaziabad   and   they   stayed   in   Ghaziabad   till   2011   and   only thereafter they came back to Delhi. PW2 as well as PW3 did not deny this fact and showed their ignorance about this fact. PW2 as well as PW3 have stated in their statement before the SDM as well as in their evidence that all the accused persons which include the accused Dinesh (since expired) and accused Sangeeta also used to harass the deceased and demand dowry. Thus,   in   case,   the   accused   Dinesh   and   Sangeeta   were   residing   in   the matrimonial   house   of   the   deceased,   PW2   as   well   as   PW3   would   have categorically denied this suggestion and stated that they had been residing in the   matrimonial   house  of  the  deceased.  Thus,  the  allegations  against  the accused Sangeeta and Dinesh that they used to harass the deceased on account   of   dowry   are   not   trustworthy.   Under   these   circumstance,   the allegations of illicit relation of accused Brijesh with accused Sangeeta are also   not   trustworthy   as   accused   Sangeeta   only   started   residing   in   the matrimonial house of the deceased  sometimes before the incident and she had not been living there since the time of marriage of deceased.

31.    It is clear from the aforesaid discussion that the evidence of all the material   witnesses   are   full   of   material   contradictions   and   full   of   material improvements and in this regard one more material improvement made by PW4 is also required to be mentioned. PW4 stated during his evidence that on 12.03.2012 i.e. one day prior to the incident, the deceased had called him and told that accused persons were harassing and beating him for dowry and she requested him to take her to her parental home. He stated that he was planning to bring her sister on the next day, however, he came to know that she   has   died.   PW4   brought   on   record   these   allegations   for   the   first   time during his evidence. He neither stated the same in his statement made u/s 161   Cr.P.C   nor   his   aforesaid   statement   was   corroborated   by     PW3. Furthermore, the allegations regarding the said call have not been proved by the prosecution on record by cogent evidence. As per PW4, the deceased had   made   a   call   to   him,   however,   neither   the   number   from   which   the deceased had called him nor the telephone number of PW4 upon which the said call was made, have been brought on record by PW4 during evidence. Furthermore, PW4 did not state the said fact in her statement Ex.PW4/DA made to the police and therefore, same is not trustworthy as it cannot be assumed that PW4 would fail to mention such a material fact to the police at the time of making his statement regarding the incident. It also cannot be assumed that in case, the PW4 would have received the said call he would not have discussed the same with other family members. Neither PW2 nor PW3 mentioned about the said call to the SDM in their statements. Thus, firstly, the allegations regarding the call are not trustworthy at all as firstly, PW4 did not state the same before the police and secondly, his allegations FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 28 of 30 qua the call were supported by any other family member i.e. PW2 and PW3 at the time of their first statement and thirdly, PW4 has not brought on record the necessary particulars of the mobile numbers and their CDR to support his allegations qua the call.

32.   Furthermore, as per PW4, the accused persons had beaten up the deceased one day prior to the incident, however, the allegations of beating and harassment are not finding support from the medical evidence also. The prosecution   has   brought   on   record   the   PM   report   of   the   deceased   as Ex.PW1/A and it is clear from the postmortem report that the deceased had suffered injury marks due to hanging and there were no other external injury marks on the body of deceased. Thus, the medical evidence available on record doesn't prove the allegations of beating or any kind of atrocity on the deceased soon before the incident. 

33.  After analyzing the record and the testimony of the material witnesses, it is clear that there are material contradictions in regard to each and every material   allegation   made   by   the   complainant   regarding   dowry   demand harassment and the statement of the family members of the deceased are not in consonance with each other qua the dowry demand and harassment and each and every family member has made deliberate improvements in his/her   statement   made   before   the   court   and   this   fact   also   makes   the testimonies   of   the   family   members   of   the   deceased   unreliable   and untrustworthy. 

34.  It is clear from the record as well as evidence led by the prosecution that the allegations made by the family members of the deceased regarding FIR No.66/12 State vs Brijesh  @ Pintoo etc 29 of 30 dowry demand are unreliable for the aforesaid discussions  and furthermore the   allegations   made   by   them   are   general   in   nature   and   lacks   material particulars. They have not specified any date or time regarding the demand and harassment.

35.   Thus,   prosecution   has   failed   to   establish   the   charges   of   dowry demand and harassment of the deceased in connection with dowry demand either through ocular evidence or through the medical evidence.  Thus, it is held   that   prosecution   has   failed   to   prove   all   the   necessary   ingredients   of section 498A/304B/34 IPC. 

CONCLUSION

36.   Keeping   in   view   of   these   facts   and   circumstances,   it   is   held   that prosecution   has   failed   to   prove   the   allegation   of   dowry   demand   and harassment, thus, all three accused are hereby acquitted of the charges u/s 498A/304B/302/34 of IPC.
