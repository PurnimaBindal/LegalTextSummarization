Hon'ble Ravindra Maithani, J. (Oral)

Applicant Smt. Sumati Devi Thapliyal is in judicial custody in Case Crime/FIR No. 20 of 2022, under Sections 304-B, 498-A, 302, 201, 315, 120B read with 34 IPC, Police Station Dharasu, District Uttarkashi. She has sought her release on bail.

2. Heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the record.

3. According to the FIR, the deceased and the applicant were married in the month of April, 2021. After the marriage, the applicant and the co-accused mentally and physically harassed the deceased for the demand of dowry. On 05.05.2022, the deceased died. The FIR records that her jewellery had broken and there were marks of injury on her neck. She was pregnant by 6 months.

2

4. Learned counsel for the applicant would submit that there has been no specific demand of dowry against the applicant. There is no evidence to suggest that soon before her death, the deceased was subjected to cruelty or harassment for or in connection with demand of dowry. Learned counsel would refer to the statement of the informant given to the Investigating Officer, where he has stated that they have assured the husband and in-laws of the deceased that they would financially assist them for opening a pharmacy shop of the deceased.

5. Learned counsel has referred to the judgment passed in the case of Appasaheb and another Vs. State of Maharashtra, (2007) 9 SCC 721 to argue that financial assistance never amounts to dowry.

6. On the other hand, learned State Counsel would submit that the witnesses have supported the prosecution case during investigation.

7. The judgment in the case of Appasaheb (supra) has further been clarified in various judgments, including the judgment in the case of Rajindra Singh Vs. State of Punjab (2015) 6 SCC 477. In fact, in the case of Jatinder Kumar Vs. State of Haryana, (2020) 16 SCC 721, the Hon'ble Supreme Court has analysed the law and held in para 9 as hereunder:- 3

"9. But the view of the Court reflected in that judgment that seeking financial assistance would not per se constitute demand for dowry has been rejected by a later judgment of a three-Judge Bench of this Court in Rajinder Singh v. State of Punjab [Rajinder Singh v. State of Punjab, (2015) 6 SCC 477 : (2015) 3 SCC (Cri) 225] . Upon considering Appasaheb [Appasaheb v. State of Maharashtra, (2007) 9 SCC 721 : (2007) 3 SCC (Cri) 468] and certain other authorities, it was held in Rajinder Singh [Rajinder Singh v. State of Punjab, (2015) 6 SCC 477 : (2015) 3 SCC (Cri) 225] : (Rajinder Singh case [Rajinder Singh v. State of Punjab, (2015) 6 SCC 477 : (2015) 3 SCC (Cri) 225] , SCC p. 491, para 20) "20. Given that the statute with which we are dealing must be given a fair, pragmatic, and common sense interpretation so as to fulfil the object sought to be achieved by Parliament, we feel that the judgment in Appasaheb case [Appasaheb v. State of Maharashtra, (2007) 9 SCC 721 : (2007) 3 SCC (Cri) 468] followed by the judgment of Vipin Jaiswal [Vipin Jaiswal v. State of A.P., (2013) 3 SCC 684 : (2013) 2 SCC (Cri) 15] do not state the law correctly. We, therefore, declare that any money or property or valuable security demanded by any of the persons mentioned in Section 2 of the Dowry Prohibition Act, at or before or at any time after the marriage which is reasonably connected to the death of a married woman, would necessarily be in connection with or in relation to the marriage unless, the facts of a 4 given case clearly and unequivocally point otherwise."

8. The applicant is mother-in-law of the deceased. The witnesses have stated about the dowry. In fact, it has been told to the Investigating Office that after the marriage, the applicant and her family members were not happy with the simple T.V. and they wanted a smart TV. Therefore, TV was substituted. The informant in answer to the last question has revealed that when after marriage the deceased was harassed for the demand of dowry, they assured to give some money to assist them in opening the pharmacy shop for the deceased. Is it directly related to the death of the deceased? The post mortem report reveals that it is the case of strangulation. Who killed the deceased in her house? As stated, the witnesses have stated about the demand of dowry.

9. Having considered the entirety of facts, this Court is of the view that the bail application deserves to be rejected.

10. The bail application is rejected.
