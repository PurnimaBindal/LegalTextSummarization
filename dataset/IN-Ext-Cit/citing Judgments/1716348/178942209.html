It has also been prayed that the re-investigation may be ordered to be conducted by respondent No.7 (CBI) as the respondents are working under the influence of accused party illegally and with mala fide intention and respondent No.1 be directed to provide security and protection to the petitioner and his family members through central agency as respondents No.2 to 6 intend to get the petitioner eliminated under the influence of the accused and to pass any other order or direction.

The brief facts as stated in the petition are that FIR No.22 dated 28.1.2013 was registered against Darshan Singh and others. The background, which led to the murder of Malo Devi, mother of the petitioner and grievous injuries dangerous to life to Tara Chand-father of the petitioner, is that the petitioner Ram Niwas had bought one plot. Accused Darshan Singh etc. wanted to illegally possess this plot. The proceedings under Section 145 Cr.P.C. were carried out upon this plot, but Shri Bhal Singh Bishnoi, the then SDM, Narwana, decided the matter against the petitioner within six days of this proceeding to give benefit to Darshan Singh etc., while he was under transfer. I have gone through the record and have heard learned counsel for the parties.

From the record, I find that as per the reply of the State, they have already presented the challan under Section 173 Cr.P.C. and it is stated that they have the right to make further investigation under Section 173(8) Cr.P.C. as per law even without permission of the Court. It is clearly stated in the reply that they are not going to reinvestigate the matter in the present case. It is denied that the local/State Police is playing in the hands of the accused persons. It is also submitted in the reply that adequate security has already been provided to the petitioner and his family members to protect their persons and property at state expenses. It is further stated that the Police is conducting further investigation in a fair, proper, impartial and transparent manner in accordance with the law.

First of all in the present case, after the completion of investigation, challan has been presented in the Court as is clear from the reply and after framing of the charge, the case has been fixed for Parmar Harpal Singh 2014.02.21 10:22 I attest to the accuracy and integrity of this document Chandigarh Cr. Misc. No.M-20410 of 2013 (O&M) [5] prosecution evidence. Therefore, the question of re-investigation from another agency does not arise. Otherwise also, as per law neither the senior Police Officers nor the Court or even the Higher Court can order the re-investigation. Only further investigation can be done under Section 173(8) Cr.P.C. As per the reply, the Police is further investigating the case. In the reply, it has been stated that the Police will make further investigation only as per law and it will be proper and impartial. At this stage, there is nothing to presume as to what will be the result of further investigation. It is stated that the permission has already been taken from the Sessions Judge for further investigation and that order is not under challenge. Again, there is no averments in the present petition that in the garb of the order of the learned Sessions Judge for further investigation, the Police wanted to exonerate the present accused. Even if any report is presented under Section 173(8) Cr.P.C. by the Police after further investigation, it is only for the trial Court to look into that report and to see whether that investigation is further investigation or it amounts to re- investigation.

It is argued by the learned counsel for the petitioner that the trial is not going on. Rather, the trial Court is awaiting the report of further investigation. On this argument, I find that the trial Court need not to wait for the further investigation report. The trial Court can continue with the proceedings. It is for the investigating agency/Police whether to submit report under Section 173(8) Cr.P.C. after making further investigation or not. Therefore, the trial Court should continue with the Parmar Harpal Singh 2014.02.21 10:22 I attest to the accuracy and integrity of this document Chandigarh Cr. Misc. No.M-20410 of 2013 (O&M) [6] proceedings and if any report is filed, then to consider it as per law.

Learned counsel for the petitioner placed reliance on the judgments of Hon'ble Supreme Court in MCD v. State of Delhi and another, 2005 SCC (Cri.) 1322 and State of Punjab v. Davinder Pal Singh Bhullar and others, 2012(1) RCR (Cr.) 126. I have gone through the law laid down in these judgments. These cases having distinguished facts will not apply in the present case. Learned counsel for the petitioner further placed reliance on the judgments of Hon'ble Supreme Court in Samaj Parivartan Samudaya and others v. State of Karnataka and others, 2012(3) RCR (Cr.) 788; Zahira Habibulla H. Sheikh and another v. State of Gujarat and others, 2004 (2) RCR (Cr.) 836 and Ramachandran v. R. Udhayakumar and others, 2008 (3) RCR (Cr.) 47. In these cases mainly it is stated that further investigation can be conducted under Section 173(8) Cr.P.C. and re-investigation cannot be conducted.

Therefore, from the above discussion, I find no merit in the present petition and the same is dismissed. January 15, 2014. (Inderjit Singh) Judge *hsp* Parmar Harpal Singh 2014.02.21 10:22 I attest to the accuracy and integrity of this document Chandigarh
